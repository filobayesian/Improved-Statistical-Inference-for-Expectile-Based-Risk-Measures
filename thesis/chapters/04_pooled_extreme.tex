\chapter{Pooled extreme expectiles}
\label{ch:pooled-extreme}

This chapter develops the pooled extreme-expectile estimator studied in the
thesis. We work in the finite-\(m\) distributed iid setting of
\Cref{sec:bg:pool-distrib}: each machine contains observations from the same
continuous heavy-tailed loss distribution, and the target is the common
marginal expectile \(\expectile{\tau_n}\). In this setting the population
target is unambiguous, and the pooled extreme-quantile component can be
handled through the distributed pooled-Weissman theorem of
\textcite[Corollary~8]{DaouiaPadoanStupfler2021}.

The construction combines the geometrically pooled Weissman estimator with the
asymptotic bridge between high expectiles and high quantiles. The role of the
chapter is to show, from an exact log decomposition, which terms remain visible
on the pooled-Weissman scale. Under the bridge-rate condition imposed below,
the first-order law is inherited from the pooled extreme-quantile component.
Accordingly, first-order weight optimisation concerns the
Daouia--Padoan--Stupfler weights in the pooled-Weissman part.

\section{Estimator and exact decomposition}
\label{sec:pool-ext:estimator}

Let \(p_n=1-\tau_n\), \(n=\sum_{j=1}^{m}n_j\), \(k=\sum_{j=1}^{m}k_j\), and
\[
  \ell_n=\log\!\left(\frac{k}{np_n}\right).
\]
For each machine \(j\), let \(\hat\gamma_j(k_j)\) be the local Hill estimator
and
\[
  \hat q_j^\star(1-p_n\mid k_j)
  =
  \left(\frac{k_j}{n_jp_n}\right)^{\hat\gamma_j(k_j)}
  X_{n_j-k_j:n_j,j}
\]
be the local Weissman estimator. For an affine weight vector
\(\bm\omega^\top\bm 1=1\), the geometrically pooled Weissman estimator is
\[
  \hatWei_n(1-p_n\mid\bm\omega)
  =
  \prod_{j=1}^{m}
  \big[\hat q_j^\star(1-p_n\mid k_j)\big]^{\omega_j}.
\]
For another affine weight vector \(\bm\nu^\top\bm 1=1\), define the pooled
Hill estimator used in the outer bridge by
\[
  \hat\gamma_n(\bm\nu)
  =
  \sum_{j=1}^{m}\nu_j\hat\gamma_j(k_j).
\]
The two-weight pooled extreme-expectile estimator is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:estimator}
  \widehat\xi_{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}(\bm\nu,\bm\omega)
  =
  \psifn\!\big(\hat\gamma_n(\bm\nu)\big)\,
  \hatWei_n(1-p_n\mid\bm\omega),
  \qquad
  \psifn(\gamma)=(\gamma^{-1}-1)^{-\gamma}.
\end{equation}
The vector \(\bm\omega\) weights the pooled-Weissman quantile estimator. The
vector \(\bm\nu\) weights only the Hill estimator appearing in the outer
expectile bridge. The diagonal construction
\(\bm\nu=\bm\omega\) is a special case, not a first-order necessity.

\begin{lemma}[Exact log decomposition]
\label{lem:pool-ext:decomposition}
Let \(Q(1-p_n)\) denote the common marginal quantile and let
\(\expectile{\tau_n}\) denote the corresponding expectile. Then
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:decomposition}
  \log
  \frac{
    \widehat\xi_{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}(\bm\nu,\bm\omega)
  }{\expectile{\tau_n}}
  =
  A_n(\bm\omega)+B_n(\bm\nu)-C_n,
\end{equation}
where
\[
  A_n(\bm\omega)
  =
  \log\frac{\hatWei_n(1-p_n\mid\bm\omega)}{Q(1-p_n)},
  \qquad
  B_n(\bm\nu)
  =
  \log\psifn\!\big(\hat\gamma_n(\bm\nu)\big)-\log\psifn(\gamma),
\]
and
\[
  C_n
  =
  \log
  \frac{\expectile{\tau_n}}{\psifn(\gamma)Q(1-p_n)}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
By the definition of the estimator,
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \log
  \frac{
    \widehat\xi_{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}(\bm\nu,\bm\omega)
  }{\expectile{\tau_n}}
  &=
  \log
  \frac{
    \psifn(\hat\gamma_n(\bm\nu))
    \hatWei_n(1-p_n\mid\bm\omega)
  }{\expectile{\tau_n}}  \\
  &=
  \log\psifn(\hat\gamma_n(\bm\nu))
  +
  \log\hatWei_n(1-p_n\mid\bm\omega)
  -
  \log\expectile{\tau_n}.
\end{aligned}
\]
Insert and subtract the two deterministic quantities
\(\log Q(1-p_n)\) and \(\log\psifn(\gamma)\). This gives
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \log
  \frac{
    \widehat\xi_{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}(\bm\nu,\bm\omega)
  }{\expectile{\tau_n}}
  &=
  \{\log\hatWei_n(1-p_n\mid\bm\omega)-\log Q(1-p_n)\}  \\
  &\quad
  +
  \{\log\psifn(\hat\gamma_n(\bm\nu))-\log\psifn(\gamma)\}  \\
  &\quad
  +
  \{\log\psifn(\gamma)+\log Q(1-p_n)-\log\expectile{\tau_n}\}.
\end{aligned}
\]
The first bracket is \(A_n(\bm\omega)\), the second bracket is
\(B_n(\bm\nu)\), and the third bracket equals
\[
  \log
  \frac{\psifn(\gamma)Q(1-p_n)}{\expectile{\tau_n}}
  =
  -\log
  \frac{\expectile{\tau_n}}{\psifn(\gamma)Q(1-p_n)}
  =
  -C_n.
\]
Combining the three brackets yields \eqref{eq:pool-ext:decomposition}.
\end{proof}

\section{A deterministic two-weight theorem}
\label{sec:pool-ext:deterministic}

The following theorem gives the first-order limit under a rate condition that
makes the deterministic population gap between expectiles and quantiles
negligible on the pooled-Weissman scale.

\begin{theorem}[Pooled extreme expectile, deterministic weights]
\label{thm:pool-ext:deterministic}
Let \(m\) be fixed. Suppose the observations across and within machines are
iid copies of a common continuous loss variable \(X\). Let
\(U(t)=Q(1-1/t)\) be the common tail quantile, and assume
\(\mathcal C_2(\gamma,\rho,A)\) with \(0<\gamma<1\) and \(\rho<0\). Assume
\(\E X^{-}<\infty\) and \(U(t)>0\) for all sufficiently large \(t\).

Let \(n=\sum_{j=1}^{m}n_j\), \(k=\sum_{j=1}^{m}k_j\), and assume
\[
  \frac{n_1}{n_j}\to b_j\in(0,\infty),
  \qquad
  \frac{k_1}{k_j}\to c_j\in(0,\infty),
\]
with \(b_1=c_1=1\). Suppose
\[
  k\to\infty,
  \qquad
  \frac{k}{n}\to0,
  \qquad
  \sqrt{k}\,A(n/k)\to\lambda\in\R.
\]
Let \(p_n=1-\tau_n\), \(\ell_n=\log\{k/(np_n)\}\), and assume the extreme
Weissman regime
\[
  p_n\to0,
  \qquad
  \frac{k}{np_n}\to\infty,
  \qquad
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}\to\infty.
\]
Finally impose the expectile-bridge rate condition
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:eta}
  \eta_n
  =
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n Q(1-p_n)}
  \to0.
\end{equation}
For deterministic affine weights
\(\bm\nu^\top\bm 1=1\) and \(\bm\omega^\top\bm 1=1\), the estimator
\eqref{eq:pool-ext:estimator} satisfies
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:log-limit}
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}
  \log
  \frac{
    \widehat\xi_{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}(\bm\nu,\bm\omega)
  }{\expectile{\tau_n}}
  \;\todist\;
  \mathcal N(B_{\bm\omega},V_{\bm\omega}),
\end{equation}
where
\[
  B_{\bm\omega}
  =
  \frac{\lambda}{1-\rho}
  \sum_{j=1}^{m}d_j^\rho\omega_j,
  \qquad
  V_{\bm\omega}
  =
  \gamma^2
  \left(\sum_{j=1}^{m}c_j^{-1}\right)
  \left(\sum_{j=1}^{m}c_j\omega_j^2\right),
\]
and
\[
  d_j
  =
  \frac{c_j}{b_j}\,
  \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{m}c_i^{-1}}{\sum_{i=1}^{m}b_i^{-1}}.
\]
Moreover,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:relative-limit}
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}
  \left(
    \frac{
      \widehat\xi_{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}(\bm\nu,\bm\omega)
    }{\expectile{\tau_n}}
    -1
  \right)
  \;\todist\;
  \mathcal N(B_{\bm\omega},V_{\bm\omega}).
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Start from the exact identity
\eqref{eq:pool-ext:decomposition}. We treat the three terms separately on the
scale \(s_n=\sqrt{k}/\ell_n\).

For \(A_n(\bm\omega)\), the common-marginal pooled-Weissman theorem of
\textcite[Corollary~8]{DaouiaPadoanStupfler2021} gives
\[
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}
  \left(
    \frac{\hatWei_n(1-p_n\mid\bm\omega)}{Q(1-p_n)}-1
  \right)
  \;\todist\;
  \mathcal N(B_{\bm\omega},V_{\bm\omega}).
\]
Set
\[
  x_n
  =
  \frac{\hatWei_n(1-p_n\mid\bm\omega)}{Q(1-p_n)}-1.
\]
The condition \(\sqrt{k}/\ell_n\to\infty\) implies
\(x_n=O_P(\ell_n/\sqrt{k})=o_P(1)\). Hence
\[
  \log(1+x_n)=x_n+O_P(x_n^2)
\]
and
\[
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}
  \{\log(1+x_n)-x_n\}
  =
  O_P\!\left(\frac{\ell_n}{\sqrt{k}}\right)
  =
  o_P(1).
\]
Therefore
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:A-limit}
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}A_n(\bm\omega)
  \;\todist\;
  \mathcal N(B_{\bm\omega},V_{\bm\omega}).
\end{equation}

For \(B_n(\bm\nu)\), write
\[
  g(x)=\log\psifn(x)=-x\log(x^{-1}-1).
\]
On every compact subinterval of \((0,1)\), \(g\) is twice continuously
differentiable, with
\[
  g'(x)=m(x)
  =
  \frac{1}{1-x}-\log(x^{-1}-1),
  \qquad
  g''(x)
  =
  \frac{1}{(1-x)^2}+\frac{1}{1-x}+\frac{1}{x}.
\]
By the pooled Hill CLT in \Cref{thm:bg:pool-hill},
\(\hat\gamma_n(\bm\nu)-\gamma=O_P(k^{-1/2})\) and
\(\hat\gamma_n(\bm\nu)\toprob\gamma\). Hence, with probability tending to one,
the line segment between \(\hat\gamma_n(\bm\nu)\) and \(\gamma\) lies in a fixed
compact subset of \((0,1)\). Taylor's theorem on that event gives
\[
  B_n(\bm\nu)
  =
  m(\gamma)\{\hat\gamma_n(\bm\nu)-\gamma\}
  +
  O_P\!\left(\{\hat\gamma_n(\bm\nu)-\gamma\}^2\right)
  =
  m(\gamma)\{\hat\gamma_n(\bm\nu)-\gamma\}+O_P(k^{-1}).
\]
Consequently
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:B-negligible}
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}B_n(\bm\nu)
  =
  \frac{m(\gamma)}{\ell_n}
  \sqrt{k}\{\hat\gamma_n(\bm\nu)-\gamma\}
  +
  O_P\!\left(\frac{1}{\ell_n\sqrt{k}}\right)
  =
  o_P(1),
\end{equation}
because \(\ell_n\to\infty\). If \(m(\gamma)=0\), the displayed conclusion
follows from the quadratic remainder alone.

It remains to control \(C_n\). Put
\[
  a_n=A(1/p_n),
  \qquad
  b_n=Q(1-p_n)^{-1},
  \qquad
  \alpha_\gamma=\gamma(\gamma^{-1}-1)^\gamma,
  \qquad
  \mu=\E X.
\]
The second-order expectile--quantile expansion
\eqref{eq:bg:c-gamma-rho} gives, after dividing by \(\psifn(\gamma)\),
\[
  \Delta_n
  \coloneqq
  \frac{\expectile{\tau_n}}{\psifn(\gamma)Q(1-p_n)}-1
  =
  c(\gamma,\rho)a_n+\alpha_\gamma\mu b_n
  +o(|a_n|)+o(b_n).
\]
Since \(\Delta_n\to0\),
\[
  C_n=\log(1+\Delta_n)=\Delta_n+O(\Delta_n^2).
\]
The regular variation of \(A\), the strict inequality \(\rho<0\), and
\[
  \frac{1/p_n}{n/k}=\frac{k}{np_n}\to\infty
\]
imply
\[
  \frac{A(1/p_n)}{A(n/k)}
  =
  \left(\frac{k}{np_n}\right)^\rho\{1+o(1)\}
  \to0.
\]
Together with \(\sqrt{k}A(n/k)\to\lambda\), this yields
\[
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}a_n\to0.
\]
The imposed bridge-rate condition \eqref{eq:pool-ext:eta} is exactly
\[
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}b_n\to0.
\]
Therefore all linear terms in \(C_n\) are \(o(1)\) after multiplication by
\(\sqrt{k}/\ell_n\). The logarithmic square remainder is also negligible, since
\[
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}(|a_n|+b_n)^2\to0
\]
follows from
\((\sqrt{k}/\ell_n)|a_n|\to0\), \((\sqrt{k}/\ell_n)b_n\to0\), and
\(|a_n|+b_n\to0\). Hence
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:C-negligible}
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}C_n\to0.
\end{equation}

Combining \eqref{eq:pool-ext:A-limit}, \eqref{eq:pool-ext:B-negligible}, and
\eqref{eq:pool-ext:C-negligible} in the exact decomposition gives
\eqref{eq:pool-ext:log-limit} by Slutsky's theorem.

For the relative-error statement, define
\[
  L_n=
  \log
  \frac{
    \widehat\xi_{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}(\bm\nu,\bm\omega)
  }{\expectile{\tau_n}}.
\]
The log limit implies \(L_n=O_P(\ell_n/\sqrt{k})=o_P(1)\). Therefore
\[
  e^{L_n}-1=L_n+O_P(L_n^2)
\]
and
\[
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}\{e^{L_n}-1-L_n\}
  =
  O_P\!\left(\frac{\ell_n}{\sqrt{k}}\right)
  =
  o_P(1).
\]
This proves \eqref{eq:pool-ext:relative-limit}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[What the theorem does and does not identify]
\label{rem:pool-ext:nu-unidentified}
The limit in \Cref{thm:pool-ext:deterministic} depends on
\(\bm\omega\), the geometric pooled-Weissman weights, but not on
\(\bm\nu\), the bridge-Hill weights. Thus \(\bm\nu\) is first-order
unidentified in the present regime. The diagonal estimator
\(\bm\nu=\bm\omega\) is a permissible deterministic convention, and the
practical convention used for plug-in inference below is
\[
  \bm\nu_n^k=\left(\frac{k_1}{k},\ldots,\frac{k_m}{k}\right)^\top.
\]
Neither convention is an optimality result for \(\bm\nu\). Any optimal choice
of bridge-Hill weights would require a lower-order theory not developed here.
\end{remark}

\section{Weight consequences}
\label{sec:pool-ext:weights}

The preceding theorem determines the relevant first-order weight criterion.
The leading bias and variance coincide with those of the
Daouia--Padoan--Stupfler pooled-Weissman component in the distributed
common-marginal case. Define
\[
  \Bc^{\mathrm{dist}}
  =
  \frac{\lambda}{1-\rho}
  (d_1^\rho,\ldots,d_m^\rho)^\top,
  \qquad
  \Vc^{\mathrm{dist}}
  =
  \gamma^2
  \left(\sum_{i=1}^{m}c_i^{-1}\right)
  \operatorname{diag}(c_1,\ldots,c_m).
\]
Then \(B_{\bm\omega}=\bm\omega^\top\Bc^{\mathrm{dist}}\) and
\(V_{\bm\omega}=\bm\omega^\top\Vc^{\mathrm{dist}}\bm\omega\).

\begin{corollary}[First-order criteria for \(\bm\omega\)]
\label{cor:pool-ext:omega-criteria}
Under the assumptions of \Cref{thm:pool-ext:deterministic}, the
first-order variance criterion for the pooled extreme-expectile estimator is
\[
  \bm\omega^\top\Vc^{\mathrm{dist}}\bm\omega,
\]
and the first-order AMSE criterion is
\[
  (\bm\omega^\top\Bc^{\mathrm{dist}})^2
  +
  \bm\omega^\top\Vc^{\mathrm{dist}}\bm\omega,
\]
both under the affine constraint \(\bm\omega^\top\bm 1=1\). Hence the
deterministic/oracle variance- and AMSE-optimal formulas of
\textcite{DaouiaPadoanStupfler2021} apply to \(\bm\omega\). In particular,
\[
  \bm\omega^{\mathrm{Var}}
  =
  \frac{(\Vc^{\mathrm{dist}})^{-1}\bm 1}
       {\bm 1^\top(\Vc^{\mathrm{dist}})^{-1}\bm 1},
\]
so, in the independent distributed case,
\[
  \omega_j^{\mathrm{Var}}
  =
  \frac{c_j^{-1}}{\sum_{i=1}^{m}c_i^{-1}},
  \qquad j=1,\ldots,m.
\]
If \(\Vc^{\mathrm{dist}}\) is positive definite, the AMSE-optimal weights are
\[
  \bm\omega^{\mathrm{AMSE}}
  =
  \frac{
    (1+(\Bc^{\mathrm{dist}})^\top
        (\Vc^{\mathrm{dist}})^{-1}\Bc^{\mathrm{dist}})
        (\Vc^{\mathrm{dist}})^{-1}\bm 1
    -
    (\bm 1^\top(\Vc^{\mathrm{dist}})^{-1}\Bc^{\mathrm{dist}})
        (\Vc^{\mathrm{dist}})^{-1}\Bc^{\mathrm{dist}}
  }{
    (1+(\Bc^{\mathrm{dist}})^\top
        (\Vc^{\mathrm{dist}})^{-1}\Bc^{\mathrm{dist}})
        (\bm 1^\top(\Vc^{\mathrm{dist}})^{-1}\bm 1)
    -
    (\bm 1^\top(\Vc^{\mathrm{dist}})^{-1}\Bc^{\mathrm{dist}})^2
  }.
\]
No first-order variance or AMSE criterion for \(\bm\nu\) is identified by
\Cref{thm:pool-ext:deterministic}.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
By \Cref{thm:pool-ext:deterministic},
\[
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}
  \log
  \frac{
    \widehat\xi_{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}(\bm\nu,\bm\omega)
  }{\expectile{\tau_n}}
  \todist
  \mathcal N(B_{\bm\omega},V_{\bm\omega}).
\]
Thus the scaled first-order mean-square criterion is
\(B_{\bm\omega}^2+V_{\bm\omega}\), and the unscaled criterion differs only by
the common factor \(\ell_n^2/k\). The same criterion applies to relative error
by \eqref{eq:pool-ext:relative-limit}. Substituting
\(B_{\bm\omega}=\bm\omega^\top\Bc^{\mathrm{dist}}\) and
\(V_{\bm\omega}=\bm\omega^\top\Vc^{\mathrm{dist}}\bm\omega\) gives exactly the
quadratic criteria recalled in \Cref{prop:bg:optimal-weights}. The variance
and AMSE formulas follow from that result.
\end{proof}

\section{Estimated pooled-Weissman weights and inference}
\label{sec:pool-ext:inference}

The deterministic theorem is the base result. Estimated weights are introduced
only in the pooled-Weissman role \(\bm\omega\), where
\textcite{DaouiaPadoanStupfler2021} already provide random-weight quantile
limits under their own consistency conditions. The bridge-Hill vector remains
deterministic in this chapter.
When the estimated weights or plug-in bias and variance objects are taken from
the Daouia--Padoan--Stupfler constructions, the source-side consistency
assumptions recalled in \Cref{sec:bg:estimated-pooling} are imposed.

The only new source input is the random-weight version of the
Daouia--Padoan--Stupfler pooled-Weissman limit: exact affine normalisation
\(\widehat{\bm\omega}_n^\top\bm 1=1\) and convergence
\(\widehat{\bm\omega}_n\toprob\bm\omega\) give the same first-order quantile
limit as the deterministic vector \(\bm\omega\). The rest of the argument is a
transfer through the exact decomposition.

\begin{corollary}[Estimated-\(\bm\omega\) transfer]
\label{cor:pool-ext:estimated-omega}
Assume the conditions of \Cref{thm:pool-ext:deterministic}, with the
deterministic \(\bm\omega\) in the estimator replaced by a random
\(\widehat{\bm\omega}_n\) satisfying the estimated-weight conditions of
\Cref{prop:bg:estimated-pooling-weights}, with limiting vector
\(\bm\omega\). Let \(\bm\nu\) be deterministic and affine, or let
\(\bm\nu=\bm\nu_n^k=(k_1/k,\ldots,k_m/k)^\top\). Then
\[
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}
  \log
  \frac{
    \widehat\xi_{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}
    (\bm\nu,\widehat{\bm\omega}_n)
  }{\expectile{\tau_n}}
  \;\todist\;
  \mathcal N(B_{\bm\omega},V_{\bm\omega}).
\]
The same limit holds on relative-error scale.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The exact decomposition becomes
\[
  \log
  \frac{
    \widehat\xi_{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}
    (\bm\nu,\widehat{\bm\omega}_n)
  }{\expectile{\tau_n}}
  =
  A_n(\widehat{\bm\omega}_n)+B_n(\bm\nu)-C_n.
\]
\Cref{prop:bg:estimated-pooling-weights} gives the random-weight
relative-error limit for the pooled-Weissman component. With
\[
  x_n(\widehat{\bm\omega}_n)
  =
  \frac{\hatWei_n(1-p_n\mid\widehat{\bm\omega}_n)}{Q(1-p_n)}-1,
\]
this gives
\[
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}x_n(\widehat{\bm\omega}_n)
  \todist
  \mathcal N(B_{\bm\omega},V_{\bm\omega}).
\]
Since \(\sqrt{k}/\ell_n\to\infty\),
\(x_n(\widehat{\bm\omega}_n)=O_P(\ell_n/\sqrt{k})=o_P(1)\), and the
relative-to-log Taylor step used in the proof of
\Cref{thm:pool-ext:deterministic} gives
\[
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}A_n(\widehat{\bm\omega}_n)
  \todist
  \mathcal N(B_{\bm\omega},V_{\bm\omega}).
\]
The bridge-Hill term is unchanged. If \(\bm\nu\) is fixed, the proof of
\eqref{eq:pool-ext:B-negligible} applies verbatim. If
\(\bm\nu=\bm\nu_n^k\), then \(\bm\nu_n^k\) is deterministic, affine, bounded,
and convergent under the threshold-proportion assumptions; the vector pooled
Hill CLT again gives
\(\hat\gamma_n(\bm\nu_n^k)-\gamma=O_P(k^{-1/2})\), so the same Taylor bound
applies. The population bridge term \(C_n\) is deterministic and is still
controlled by \eqref{eq:pool-ext:C-negligible}. Slutsky's theorem gives the
log limit, and the final exponential Taylor step in
\Cref{thm:pool-ext:deterministic} gives the relative-error version.
\end{proof}

Thus any source-admissible Daouia--Padoan--Stupfler construction of
\(\widehat{\bm\omega}_n\) transfers in the pooled-Weissman role, including
estimated variance- and AMSE-optimal weights when their source assumptions give
\(\widehat{\bm\omega}_n\toprob\bm\omega\). This statement concerns
\(\bm\omega\) only. It does not optimise \(\bm\nu\), and it does not cover a
random bridge-Hill convention such as
\(\bm\nu=\widehat{\bm\omega}_n\).

\begin{corollary}[Plug-in studentisation and log-scale interval]
\label{cor:pool-ext:plugin-inference}
Let
\[
  \widehat\xi_{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{plug}}
  =
  \widehat\xi_{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}
  (\bm\nu_n^k,\widehat{\bm\omega}_n),
  \qquad
  \bm\nu_n^k=
  \left(\frac{k_1}{k},\ldots,\frac{k_m}{k}\right)^\top.
\]
Under the assumptions of \Cref{cor:pool-ext:estimated-omega}, let
\(\widehat{\Bc}^{\mathrm{dist}}_n\) and
\(\widehat{\Vc}^{\mathrm{dist}}_n\) be plug-in estimators satisfying
\[
  \widehat{\Bc}^{\mathrm{dist}}_n\toprob\Bc^{\mathrm{dist}},
  \qquad
  \widehat{\Vc}^{\mathrm{dist}}_n\toprob\Vc^{\mathrm{dist}}.
\]
These consistency assumptions belong to the selected
Daouia--Padoan--Stupfler plug-in route; they are not consequences of the
expectile decomposition alone. No expectile-specific bias or variance estimator
is introduced here.
Define
\[
  \widehat B_{\widehat{\bm\omega},n}
  =
  \widehat{\bm\omega}_n^\top\widehat{\Bc}^{\mathrm{dist}}_n,
  \qquad
  \widehat V_{\widehat{\bm\omega},n}
  =
  \widehat{\bm\omega}_n^\top
  \widehat{\Vc}^{\mathrm{dist}}_n
  \widehat{\bm\omega}_n,
\]
and assume \(V_{\bm\omega}>0\). Then
\[
  T_n^{\mathrm{plug}}
  =
  \frac{
    \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}
    \log\!\left(
      \frac{\widehat\xi_{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{plug}}}
           {\expectile{\tau_n}}
    \right)
    -
    \widehat B_{\widehat{\bm\omega},n}
  }{
    \sqrt{\widehat V_{\widehat{\bm\omega},n}}
  }
  \;\todist\;
  \mathcal N(0,1).
\]
Let \(z_{1-\alpha/2}\) be the standard normal quantile. On the event
\(\widehat V_{\widehat{\bm\omega},n}>0\), define
\[
  I_{n,1-\alpha}^{\mathrm{plug}}
  =
  \left[
  \widehat\xi_{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{plug}}
  \exp\!\left\{
    -\frac{\ell_n}{\sqrt{k}}
    \left(
      \widehat B_{\widehat{\bm\omega},n}
      +
      z_{1-\alpha/2}\sqrt{\widehat V_{\widehat{\bm\omega},n}}
    \right)
  \right\},
  \right.
\]
\[
  \left.
  \widehat\xi_{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{plug}}
  \exp\!\left\{
    -\frac{\ell_n}{\sqrt{k}}
    \left(
      \widehat B_{\widehat{\bm\omega},n}
      -
      z_{1-\alpha/2}\sqrt{\widehat V_{\widehat{\bm\omega},n}}
    \right)
  \right\}
  \right].
\]
Then
\[
  \Prob\!\left\{
    \expectile{\tau_n}\in I_{n,1-\alpha}^{\mathrm{plug}}
  \right\}
  \to
  1-\alpha.
\]
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
\Cref{cor:pool-ext:estimated-omega} gives
\[
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}
  \log\!\left(
    \frac{\widehat\xi_{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{plug}}}{\expectile{\tau_n}}
  \right)
  \todist
  \mathcal N(B_{\bm\omega},V_{\bm\omega}).
\]
\Cref{prop:bg:plugin-bias-variance}, applied with
\(\Bc=\Bc^{\mathrm{dist}}\) and \(\Vc=\Vc^{\mathrm{dist}}\), gives
\[
  \widehat B_{\widehat{\bm\omega},n}\toprob B_{\bm\omega},
  \qquad
  \widehat V_{\widehat{\bm\omega},n}\toprob V_{\bm\omega}.
\]
The additional expectile terms have already been absorbed in
\Cref{cor:pool-ext:estimated-omega}, so the centring and variance objects remain
the pooled-Weissman objects supplied by the source theory. Together with
\(V_{\bm\omega}>0\), the displayed convergences give the standard normal limit
by Slutsky's theorem.
Since \(\widehat V_{\widehat{\bm\omega},n}\toprob V_{\bm\omega}>0\), the event
\(\widehat V_{\widehat{\bm\omega},n}>0\) has probability tending to one.
Solving
\[
  \left|T_n^{\mathrm{plug}}\right|\le z_{1-\alpha/2}
\]
for the unknown \(\expectile{\tau_n}\) gives the displayed multiplicative
interval, so its asymptotic coverage follows from the studentised limit.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Scope of the inference corollary]
\label{rem:pool-ext:interval-scope}
The interval in \Cref{cor:pool-ext:plugin-inference} is obtained by inverting
the plug-in-centred expectile statistic above. It is therefore a consequence
of the transferred expectile limit, rather than a direct restatement of the
quantile interval in \textcite{DaouiaPadoanStupfler2021}. The centering and
variance objects remain those of the pooled-Weissman component because, under
\(\eta_n\to0\), the additional expectile-bridge terms are lower order on that
scale. The corollary is consequently conditional on the selected
Daouia--Padoan--Stupfler construction providing consistent plug-in objects for
\(B_{\bm\omega}\) and \(V_{\bm\omega}\); it does not make an additional claim
about simulations, finite-sample calibration, or optimal bridge-Hill weights.
\end{remark}
