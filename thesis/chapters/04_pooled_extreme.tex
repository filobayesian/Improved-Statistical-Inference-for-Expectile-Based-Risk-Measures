\chapter{Extrapolation to very-extreme expectile levels}
\label{ch:pool-ext}

\section{Motivation}
\label{sec:pool-ext:motivation}

The intermediate-level theory of \Cref{ch:pool-int} estimates
$\expectile{\tau_n}$ when the expected number of upper-tail observations
diverges. This chapter moves to the very-extreme regime
\[
  \tau_n^\prime \uparrow 1,
  \qquad
  n(1-\tau_n^\prime)\to c\in[0,\infty),
\]
where intermediate-level asymptotics no longer apply directly. As in
the iid theory recalled in \Cref{sec:bg:weissman-expectile}, the stable
anchor remains an intermediate expectile estimator, and the target
$\expectile{\tau_n^\prime}$ is reached through a Weissman-type power
factor.

The pooling protocol is unchanged: samples communicate only the Paper-3
Hill and Weissman primitives, with covariance information for weights
and standard errors. Hence the available intermediate anchor is the
canonical pooled QB estimator
\[
  \hatexpectile{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{pool}}
    (\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q)
  =
  \psifn\!\big(\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma)\big)
  \hatWei_n(\tau_n\mid\bm\omega_q)
\]
from \Cref{eq:pool-int:expectile-pool}. This is a consequence of the
communication protocol, not a claim that QB extrapolation dominates
procedures using expectile-specific primitives.

The role of \Cref{prop:bg:plug-in-clt}, the thesis-level plug-in
lemma adapted from \textcite[Supplement, Proposition~C.6]{DavisonPadoanStupfler2023},
is to make this transfer modular. Once an intermediate estimator and a
tail-index estimator satisfy a joint root-rate limit, the extrapolated
expectile has a very-extreme limit governed only by the tail-index
component. Chapter~3 supplies the required joint limit for
$\big(\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma),
\hatexpectile{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{pool}}(\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q)\big)$.
The rest of the chapter specifies the regime, defines the pooled lift,
and reads the limiting distribution and optimal weights from the pooled
Hill component.

\section{Extrapolation regime and input limit}
\label{sec:pool-ext:regime}

Throughout this chapter we keep the independent common-margin setup of
\Cref{sec:pool-int:setup}: fixed $m$, $n=\sum_j n_j$,
$k=\sum_j k_j$, $n_j\to\infty$, $k_j\to\infty$, $k_j/n_j\to0$, and the
proportionality conditions \eqref{eq:bg:pool-rates}. The common margin
satisfies $\mathcal C_2(\gamma,\rho,A)$ with $\gamma\in(0,1)$ and
\[
  \E X^{-}<\infty .
\]
The bias-rate limits $\sqrt{k_j}A(n_j/k_j)\to\lambda_j\in\R$ hold, the
intermediate anchor level satisfies
\[
  L_j(\tau_n)
  =
  \log\!\frac{k_j}{n_j(1-\tau_n)}
  \;\longrightarrow\;
  L_j^\star\in\R,
  \qquad j=1,\ldots,m,
\]
and the moment-negligibility condition is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:moment-rate}
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\Quant_X(\tau_n)}\;\longrightarrow\;0.
\end{equation}
All logarithms are understood on the high-probability positivity event
of \Cref{sec:pool-int:estimator}. The only strengthening relative to the
intermediate chapter is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:rho-strict}
  \rho<0.
\end{equation}
This is the strict second-order condition used in
\Cref{prop:bg:plug-in-clt} and in the pooled Weissman theorem
\Cref{thm:bg:pool-weissman}. All results below remain in the independent
common-margin distributed specialisation.

The new target level is a very-extreme sequence
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:very-extreme-level}
  \tau_n^\prime\uparrow1,
  \qquad
  n(1-\tau_n^\prime)\to c\in[0,\infty),
  \qquad
  \frac{1-\tau_n^\prime}{1-\tau_n}\to0.
\end{equation}
Write the associated Weissman log-rate as
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:ell-def}
  \ell_n
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \log\!\left(\frac{1-\tau_n}{1-\tau_n^\prime}\right).
\end{equation}
Then $\ell_n\to\infty$, and the extrapolation is not allowed to be so
far that the Weissman log-rate overtakes the available root-$k$
information:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:weissman-rate}
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}\;\longrightarrow\;\infty.
\end{equation}
The finite-log condition already implies $n(1-\tau_n)\to\infty$, so
$\tau_n$ is intermediate and $\tau_n^\prime$ is very-extreme in the
sense required by \Cref{prop:bg:plug-in-clt}. If
$\alpha_j=\lim n_j/n\in(0,1)$, then
\[
  \alpha_j
  =
  \frac{b_j^{-1}}{\sum_{i=1}^{m}b_i^{-1}},
  \qquad
  n_j(1-\tau_n^\prime)\to \alpha_jc.
\]
Thus the global very-extreme level has the expected local interpretation.

The logarithmic rate used here is also aligned with the pooled
Weissman rate in \Cref{thm:bg:pool-weissman}. The finite-log condition
and proportionality assumptions imply
\[
  \log\!\frac{k}{n(1-\tau_n)} = O(1),
\]
and hence
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:rate-alignment}
  \log\!\frac{k}{n(1-\tau_n^\prime)}
  =
  \ell_n
  +
  \log\!\frac{k}{n(1-\tau_n)}
  =
  \ell_n + O(1).
\end{equation}
Thus the pooled-Weissman logarithmic normaliser and $\ell_n$ are
asymptotically equivalent.

For the application of \Cref{prop:bg:plug-in-clt} we take
\[
  a_n=\sqrt{k},\qquad
  \hat\gamma_n=\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma),\qquad
  \widehat\xi_{\tau_n}^{\,I}
  =
  \hatexpectile{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{pool}}
    (\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q).
\]
The rate assumptions above verify the remaining rate hypotheses of that
proposition: $a_n\to\infty$, $\tau_n$ is intermediate,
$\tau_n^\prime$ is very-extreme, \eqref{eq:pool-ext:weissman-rate} gives
$a_n/\ell_n\to\infty$, \Cref{eq:pool-int:Lambda-bullet} gives
$a_nA((1-\tau_n)^{-1})\to\Lambda^\bullet$, and
\eqref{eq:pool-ext:moment-rate} gives $a_n/\Quant_X(\tau_n)\to0$.
The positivity condition holds on the log-domain event already used in
\Cref{ch:pool-int}. It remains only to record the deterministic ratio
control and the joint Gaussian input.

\begin{lemma}[Deterministic very-extreme ratio control]
\label{lem:pool-ext:det-ratio}
Under the tail and rate assumptions of this section,
\[
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}
  \left\{
    \log\expectile{\tau_n^\prime}
    - \log\expectile{\tau_n}
    - \gamma\ell_n
  \right\}
  \;\longrightarrow\;0.
\]
This lemma records, in the present notation, the deterministic
population step behind the finite-mean plug-in extrapolation result of
\textcite[Supplement, Proposition~C.6]{DavisonPadoanStupfler2023}. The proof of
that proposition reduces the argument to the same log-decomposition as
\textcite[Theorem~5]{DaouiaGirardStupfler2020}; the control is used
here as a source-paper ingredient, not as a new contribution of the
thesis.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
See \Cref{app:proofs:pool-ext-det-ratio}.
\end{proof}

It remains to record the exact intermediate Gaussian input that will
be fed into the plug-in extrapolation lemma. Put
\[
  \bm D^\star \coloneqq \mathrm{diag}(\bm L^\star),
  \qquad
  \bm L^\star=(L_1^\star,\ldots,L_m^\star)^\top .
\]

\begin{proposition}[Joint input for the extrapolation step]
\label{prop:pool-ext:input-clt}
Under the assumptions stated above, for any deterministic affine weight vectors
$\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q\in\R^m$ satisfying
$\bm\omega_\gamma^\top\bm 1=\bm\omega_q^\top\bm 1=1$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:input-clt}
  \sqrt{k}
  \begin{pmatrix}
    \hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma)-\gamma \\[2pt]
    \hatexpectile{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{pool}}
      (\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q)/\expectile{\tau_n}-1
  \end{pmatrix}
  \;\todist\;
  \mathcal N\!\left(
  \begin{pmatrix}
    \bm\omega_\gamma^\top\Bc \\[2pt]
    \mu^\xi(\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q)
  \end{pmatrix},
  \begin{pmatrix}
    \bm\omega_\gamma^\top\Vc\bm\omega_\gamma
      & s_{\gamma,\xi}(\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q) \\[2pt]
    s_{\gamma,\xi}(\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q)
      & \sigma^\xi(\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q)^2
  \end{pmatrix}
  \right),
\end{equation}
where $\mu^\xi$ and $\sigma^\xi$ are given in
\eqref{eq:pool-int:mu-xi} and \eqref{eq:pool-int:sigma-xi}, and
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:gamma-xi-cross}
  s_{\gamma,\xi}(\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q)
  =
  m(\gamma)\,\bm\omega_\gamma^\top\Vc\bm\omega_\gamma
  +
  \bm\omega_\gamma^\top\bm D^\star\Vc\bm\omega_q .
\end{equation}
The same convergence holds with random weights
$(\hat{\bm\omega}_\gamma,\hat{\bm\omega}_q)$ satisfying
\[
  \hat{\bm\omega}_\gamma^\top\bm 1
  =
  \hat{\bm\omega}_q^\top\bm 1
  =
  1
  \quad\text{exactly,}
  \qquad
  \hat{\bm\omega}_\gamma\toprob\bm\omega_\gamma,
  \qquad
  \hat{\bm\omega}_q\toprob\bm\omega_q .
\]
In this estimated-weight version, the mean and covariance on the
right-hand side of \eqref{eq:pool-ext:input-clt} are evaluated at the
deterministic probability limits
$(\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q)$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
See \Cref{app:proofs:pool-ext-input-clt}.
\end{proof}

\Cref{prop:pool-ext:input-clt} is more information than the final
very-extreme theorem visibly retains. In the notation of
\Cref{prop:bg:plug-in-clt}, its first component is
\[
  \Gamma\sim
  \mathcal N\!\big(\bm\omega_\gamma^\top\Bc,\,
                   \bm\omega_\gamma^\top\Vc\bm\omega_\gamma\big),
\]
while its second component is the intermediate pooled-QB limit
$\Delta$. The division by the diverging $\ell_n$ removes $\Delta$ from
the first-order extrapolated law, so the only surviving bias is
$\bm\omega_\gamma^\top\Bc$.

\section{The pooled extrapolated expectile estimator}
\label{sec:pool-ext:estimator}

The extrapolated estimator is obtained by applying the Weissman-type
lift of \Cref{eq:bg:weissman-expectile} to the pooled intermediate
anchor of \Cref{eq:pool-int:expectile-pool}. For affine weights
$\bm\omega_{\gamma},\bm\omega_q\in\R^m$ satisfying
$\bm\omega_\gamma^\top\bm 1=\bm\omega_q^\top\bm 1=1$, define
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:estimator}
  \hatexpectile{\tau_n^\prime}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}
    (\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q)
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \left(\frac{1-\tau_n^\prime}{1-\tau_n}\right)^{
    -\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma)}
  \hatexpectile{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{pool}}
    (\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q).
\end{equation}
Although $\bm\omega_q$ affects the finite-sample anchor and the
intermediate-level component of the joint input limit, it disappears
from the first-order very-extreme CLT after normalisation by
$\sqrt{k}/\ell_n$. It remains part of the definition because different
choices of $\bm\omega_q$ give different finite-sample estimators and
higher-order terms.
Equivalently, using \Cref{eq:pool-int:expectile-pool},
\[
  \hatexpectile{\tau_n^\prime}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}
    (\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q)
  =
  \exp\{\ell_n\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma)\}\,
  \psifn\!\big(\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma)\big)\,
  \hatWei_n(\tau_n\mid\bm\omega_q),
\]
where $\ell_n$ is the log-rate in \eqref{eq:pool-ext:ell-def}. Thus
the two primitive weights remain visible: $\bm\omega_\gamma$ controls
the QB factor and extrapolation power, while $\bm\omega_q$ controls the
intermediate quantile anchor. As in \Cref{sec:pool-int:estimator}, all
log-scale and real-power expressions are read on the high-probability
positivity event; arbitrary definitions off that event do not affect
weak limits.

The construction targets the same expectile level $\tau_n^\prime$ that
appears in $\expectile{\tau_n^\prime}$; no adjusted quantile level is
introduced. The bridge
$\expectile{\tau}/\Quant_X(\tau)\to\psifn(\gamma)$ is same-level, and
\Cref{prop:bg:plug-in-clt} extrapolates the intermediate expectile from
$\tau_n$ to $\tau_n^\prime$.

At matched weights, \eqref{eq:pool-ext:estimator} is exactly the
QB plug-in applied to the pooled Weissman quantile at the
very-extreme level. Indeed, by the local Weissman identity,
\[
  \hat q_j^\star(\tau_n^\prime\mid k_j)
  =
  \left(\frac{1-\tau_n^\prime}{1-\tau_n}\right)^{
    -\hat\gamma_j(k_j)}
  \hat q_j^\star(\tau_n\mid k_j),
\]
and therefore, for every affine $\bm\omega$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:matched-weissman-identity}
  \hatWei_n(\tau_n^\prime\mid\bm\omega)
  =
  \left(\frac{1-\tau_n^\prime}{1-\tau_n}\right)^{
    -\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega)}
  \hatWei_n(\tau_n\mid\bm\omega).
\end{equation}
Combining \eqref{eq:pool-ext:matched-weissman-identity} with
\eqref{eq:pool-ext:estimator} gives the exact matched-weight identity
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:matched-expectile-identity}
  \hatexpectile{\tau_n^\prime}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}
    (\bm\omega,\bm\omega)
  =
  \psifn\!\big(\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega)\big)\,
  \hatWei_n(\tau_n^\prime\mid\bm\omega).
\end{equation}
Thus, on the matched diagonal, the Chapter~4 estimator is not a new
third primitive: it is the same pooled-Hill / pooled-Weissman QB
pipeline as in Chapter~3, evaluated after the Weissman quantile input
has been extrapolated to $\tau_n^\prime$.

\begin{remark}[Matched local-QB equivalence]
\label{rem:pool-ext:matched-local-qb}
There is also a local-QB analogue of the construction, parallel to
\Cref{eq:pool-int:expectile-B}. Define the marginal extrapolated QB
expectile on sample $j$ by
\[
  \hatexpectile{\tau_n^\prime,j}^{\star}
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \left(\frac{1-\tau_n^\prime}{1-\tau_n}\right)^{
    -\hat\gamma_j(k_j)}
  \psifn\!\big(\hat\gamma_j(k_j)\big)\,
  \hat q_j^\star(\tau_n\mid k_j)
  =
  \psifn\!\big(\hat\gamma_j(k_j)\big)\,
  \hat q_j^\star(\tau_n^\prime\mid k_j),
\]
and geometrically pool these $m$ scalars:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:local-qb-geometric}
  \hatexpectile{\tau_n^\prime}^{\mathrm{loc},\star}(\bm\omega)
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \prod_{j=1}^{m}
    \big[\hatexpectile{\tau_n^\prime,j}^{\star}\big]^{\omega_j}.
\end{equation}
The comparison with \eqref{eq:pool-ext:matched-expectile-identity}
reduces to the same $\psifn$-placement difference as in
\Cref{prop:pool-int:A-B-equiv}:
\[
  \log\!\frac{
    \hatexpectile{\tau_n^\prime}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}
      (\bm\omega,\bm\omega)}
    {\hatexpectile{\tau_n^\prime}^{\mathrm{loc},\star}(\bm\omega)}
  =
  \log\psifn\!\big(\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega)\big)
  -
  \sum_{j=1}^{m}\omega_j
    \log\psifn\!\big(\hat\gamma_j(k_j)\big),
\]
so the unscaled log-ratio is $O_p(k^{-1})$, hence negligible on both
the intermediate $\sqrt{k}$ scale and the extreme $\sqrt{k}/\ell_n$
scale. Thus \eqref{eq:pool-ext:estimator} agrees first order with the
local-QB interpretation on the matched diagonal.
\end{remark}

The matched-weight qualification is essential. If
$\bm\omega_\gamma\ne\bm\omega_q$, replacing
\eqref{eq:pool-ext:estimator} by
\[
  \psifn\!\big(\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma)\big)\,
  \hatWei_n(\tau_n^\prime\mid\bm\omega_q)
\]
changes the extrapolation exponent from
$\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma)$ to $\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_q)$. The
log-ratio contains
\[
  \ell_n\{
    \hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_q)-\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma)
  \}.
\]
After the extreme normalisation $\sqrt{k}/\ell_n$, this contributes
$\sqrt{k}\{\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_q)-\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma)\}$,
which is generally non-negligible.

\section{Main extrapolation theorem}
\label{sec:pool-ext:CLT}

We can now state the chapter's main limit theorem. It is a direct
application of the plug-in extrapolation result
\Cref{prop:bg:plug-in-clt} to the pooled intermediate input of
\Cref{prop:pool-ext:input-clt}. The theorem is intentionally
short: all expectile-specific work has already been done at the
intermediate level, and the very-extreme scale keeps only the
pooled-Hill component.

\begin{theorem}[Pooled Weissman extrapolation for extreme expectiles]
\label{thm:pool-ext:main}
Assume the setup and rate conditions of \Cref{sec:pool-ext:regime};
in particular, $\rho<0$, $\sqrt{k}/\Quant_X(\tau_n)\to0$,
$\ell_n\to\infty$ and $\sqrt{k}/\ell_n\to\infty$. Use the
positivity and log-domain convention of
\Cref{sec:pool-ext:estimator}, and the deterministic ratio control
verified in \Cref{lem:pool-ext:det-ratio}.
Let $\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q\in\R^m$ be deterministic affine
weight vectors satisfying
$\bm\omega_\gamma^\top\bm 1=\bm\omega_q^\top\bm 1=1$, and define
$\hatexpectile{\tau_n^\prime}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}
(\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q)$ by
\eqref{eq:pool-ext:estimator}. Then
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:main-clt}
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}
  \left(
    \frac{\hatexpectile{\tau_n^\prime}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}
      (\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q)}
         {\expectile{\tau_n^\prime}}
    - 1
  \right)
  \;\todist\;
  \mathcal N\!\left(
    \bm\omega_\gamma^\top\Bc,\;
    \bm\omega_\gamma^\top\Vc\bm\omega_\gamma
  \right),
\end{equation}
where $\ell_n=\log\{(1-\tau_n)/(1-\tau_n^\prime)\}$.

The same conclusion holds with estimated weights
$(\hat{\bm\omega}_\gamma,\hat{\bm\omega}_q)$, provided
\[
  \hat{\bm\omega}_\gamma^\top\bm 1
  =
  \hat{\bm\omega}_q^\top\bm 1
  =
  1
  \quad\text{exactly,}
  \qquad
  \hat{\bm\omega}_\gamma\toprob\bm\omega_\gamma,
  \qquad
  \hat{\bm\omega}_q\toprob\bm\omega_q .
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
See \Cref{app:proofs:pool-ext-main}.
\end{proof}

\Cref{thm:pool-ext:main} is the expectile analogue of the pooled
Weissman limit in the independent common-margin setting. The diverging
logarithmic distance magnifies tail-index error, while the intermediate
anchor is lower order. Thus $m(\gamma)$, $\Bc^\star$ and
$b^{\xi/\Quant}$ need not vanish; they simply do not survive the
$\sqrt{k}/\ell_n$ normalisation.

\section{First-order optimal Hill weights for extrapolation}
\label{sec:pool-ext:weights}

After normalisation by $\sqrt{k}/\ell_n$, the intermediate anchor is
first-order invisible. The limiting mean and variance are
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:mean-var}
  \mu^{\xi,\star}(\bm\omega_\gamma)
  =
  \bm\omega_\gamma^\top\Bc,
  \qquad
  \sigma^{\xi,\star}(\bm\omega_\gamma)^2
  =
  \bm\omega_\gamma^\top\Vc\bm\omega_\gamma .
\end{equation}
This is the same quadratic structure as in
\Cref{prop:bg:optimal-weights}. The first-order criterion therefore
identifies only the extrapolation exponent, namely
$\bm\omega_\gamma$; it does not identify the anchor weight
$\bm\omega_q$.

As in \Cref{prop:bg:optimal-weights}, weights are real affine weights;
no non-negativity constraint is imposed. If convex weights are required,
the solution is the corresponding constrained quadratic programme and
need not equal the closed forms below.

\begin{proposition}[First-order optimal Hill weights for extrapolated expectiles]
\label{prop:pool-ext:optimal-weights}
Assume the hypotheses of \Cref{thm:pool-ext:main}, and suppose that
$\Vc$ is positive definite.
Among affine Hill weights $\bm\omega_\gamma^\top\bm 1=1$, the
variance-optimal extrapolation weight is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:var-opt}
  \bm\omega_\gamma^{\xi,\mathrm{Var},\star}
  =
  \bm\omega^{\mathrm{Var}}
  =
  \frac{\Vc^{-1}\bm 1}{\bm 1^\top\Vc^{-1}\bm 1},
\end{equation}
and the minimum first-order variance is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:var-opt-value}
  \sigma^{\xi,\star}
    (\bm\omega_\gamma^{\xi,\mathrm{Var},\star})^2
  =
  \frac{1}{\bm 1^\top\Vc^{-1}\bm 1}.
\end{equation}
If the limiting bias is retained, the first-order AMSE criterion is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:amse-def}
  \mathrm{AMSE}^{\xi,\star}(\bm\omega_\gamma)
  =
  \big(\bm\omega_\gamma^\top\Bc\big)^2
  +
  \bm\omega_\gamma^\top\Vc\bm\omega_\gamma,
  \qquad
  \bm\omega_\gamma^\top\bm 1=1,
\end{equation}
whose unique minimiser is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:amse-opt}
  \bm\omega_\gamma^{\xi,\mathrm{AMSE},\star}
  =
  \bm\omega^{\mathrm{AMSE}}
  =
  \frac{(1 + \Bc^\top\Vc^{-1}\Bc)\,\Vc^{-1}\bm 1
        - (\bm 1^\top\Vc^{-1}\Bc)\,\Vc^{-1}\Bc}
       {(1 + \Bc^\top\Vc^{-1}\Bc)(\bm 1^\top\Vc^{-1}\bm 1)
        - (\bm 1^\top\Vc^{-1}\Bc)^2}.
\end{equation}
The corresponding minimum AMSE is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:amse-opt-value}
  \mathrm{AMSE}^{\xi,\star}
    (\bm\omega_\gamma^{\xi,\mathrm{AMSE},\star})
  =
  \frac{1+\Bc^\top\Vc^{-1}\Bc}
       {(1 + \Bc^\top\Vc^{-1}\Bc)(\bm 1^\top\Vc^{-1}\bm 1)
        - (\bm 1^\top\Vc^{-1}\Bc)^2}.
\end{equation}
The denominator in \eqref{eq:pool-ext:amse-opt}--\eqref{eq:pool-ext:amse-opt-value}
is strictly positive.
The quantile-anchor weight $\bm\omega_q$ is not identified by either
first-order criterion: for the variance objective, every pair
$(\bm\omega^{\mathrm{Var}},\bm\nu)$ with
$\bm\nu^\top\bm 1=1$ has the same first-order variance, and for the
AMSE objective the same is true of every pair
$(\bm\omega^{\mathrm{AMSE}},\bm\nu)$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
See \Cref{app:proofs:pool-ext-optimal-weights}.
\end{proof}

In the independent common-distribution distributed setting used
throughout the contribution chapters, $\Rjl\equiv0$ for $j\ne\ell$
and \Cref{eq:bg:V_c-diagonal} gives a diagonal covariance matrix.
Consequently \eqref{eq:pool-ext:var-opt} reduces to
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:var-opt-distrib}
  \omega_{\gamma,j}^{\xi,\mathrm{Var},\star}
  =
  \left(\sum_{i=1}^{m}c_i^{-1}\right)^{-1}c_j^{-1}
  \;\sim\;
  \frac{k_j}{k},
  \qquad j=1,\ldots,m.
\end{equation}
Since $c_j^{-1}$ is proportional to the limiting effective sample size,
the variance-optimal distributed rule is again effective-sample-size
weighting. If $\Bc$ is collinear with $\bm 1$ --- for instance under
undersmoothing, or under equal effective fractions in the common-margin
regime --- then $\bm\omega_\gamma^\top\Bc$ is constant on the affine
constraint set, so
$\bm\omega_\gamma^{\xi,\mathrm{AMSE},\star}
=\bm\omega_\gamma^{\xi,\mathrm{Var},\star}$.

This is where Chapter~4 differs from \Cref{sec:pool-int:weights}. The
intermediate AMSE contains $m(\gamma)$, $\bm L^\star$, $\Bc^\star$ and
$b^{\xi/\Quant}$, whereas the very-extreme first-order AMSE contains
only $\Bc$ and $\Vc$. Outside collapse regimes, Chapter~4 AMSE weights
therefore need not be the Chapter~3 expectile AMSE weights.

The non-identification of $\bm\omega_q$ is only first-order. The anchor
weight still changes the finite-sample point estimator and may affect
higher-order accuracy. A natural canonical representative is the
matched choice
\[
  \bm\omega_q=\bm\omega_\gamma,
\]
with $\bm\omega_\gamma$ chosen as in \eqref{eq:pool-ext:var-opt} or
\eqref{eq:pool-ext:amse-opt}. This is an implementation convention, not
a first-order optimality consequence. It keeps the estimator in the
matched Weissman form
$\psifn(\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma))\,
\hatWei_n(\tau_n^\prime\mid\bm\omega_\gamma)$ and gives a single
default for \Cref{sec:pool-ext:CI}.

\section{Bias-reduction}
\label{sec:pool-ext:bias-reduction}

On the $\sqrt{k}/\ell_n$ scale, \Cref{thm:pool-ext:main} retains only
the pooled-Hill bias
\[
  \mu^{\xi,\star}(\bm\omega_\gamma)
  =
  \bm\omega_\gamma^\top\Bc .
\]
The intermediate QB bias terms enter only the second coordinate of
\Cref{prop:pool-ext:input-clt} and are divided out by the diverging
$\ell_n$. Thus very-extreme bias reduction is inherited directly from
the tail-index correction of \textcite[\S2.2]{DaouiaPadoanStupfler2021}.

Assume that a consistent estimator of the limiting Hill-bias vector is
available,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:Bc-hat-consistency}
  \hatBc \toprob \Bc .
\end{equation}
This is an additional second-order calibration; it does not follow from
$\mathcal C_2(\gamma,\rho,A)$ alone.
For any affine Hill-weight vector $\bm\omega_\gamma^\top\bm 1=1$,
define the bias-reduced pooled tail-index estimator
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:bias-reduced-gamma}
  \overline\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma)
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma)
  -
  \frac{\bm\omega_\gamma^\top\hatBc}{\sqrt{k}} .
\end{equation}
The associated bias-reduced extrapolated expectile keeps the same
intermediate anchor as \eqref{eq:pool-ext:estimator}, but replaces the
extrapolation exponent by \eqref{eq:pool-ext:bias-reduced-gamma}:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:bias-reduced-estimator}
  \hatexpectile{\tau_n^\prime}^{\mathrm{pool},\star,\mathrm{bc}}
    (\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q)
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \left(\frac{1-\tau_n^\prime}{1-\tau_n}\right)^{
    -\overline\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma)}
  \hatexpectile{\tau_n}^{\mathrm{pool}}
    (\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q).
\end{equation}
For estimated weights, the same formula is used after substituting
$(\hat{\bm\omega}_\gamma,\hat{\bm\omega}_q)$ for
$(\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q)$. Only the exponent is corrected because
only the exponent contributes at the $\sqrt{k}/\ell_n$ scale. Replacing
the occurrence of
$\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma)$ inside the intermediate QB factor
$\psifn(\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma))$ by
$\overline\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma)$, on the same high-probability
compact event used for Taylor expansion of $\log\psifn$, would change
\eqref{eq:pool-ext:bias-reduced-estimator} by a log-ratio of order
$O_p(k^{-1/2})$, hence by $o_p(1)$ after multiplication by
$\sqrt{k}/\ell_n$. The minimal correction above is therefore enough
for the first-order theorem and introduces no new log-domain
restriction.

\begin{corollary}[Bias-reduced extrapolated expectile CLT]
\label{cor:pool-ext:bias-reduced-clt}
Assume the hypotheses of \Cref{thm:pool-ext:main}, and assume
\eqref{eq:pool-ext:Bc-hat-consistency}; in particular
$\ell_n\to\infty$ and $\sqrt{k}/\ell_n\to\infty$, and the positivity
convention of \Cref{sec:pool-ext:estimator} is in force. Let
$\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q\in\R^m$ be deterministic affine weight
vectors satisfying
$\bm\omega_\gamma^\top\bm 1=\bm\omega_q^\top\bm 1=1$.
Then the estimator
\eqref{eq:pool-ext:bias-reduced-estimator} satisfies
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:bias-reduced-clt}
  \frac{\sqrt{k}}{\ell_n}
  \left(
    \frac{
      \hatexpectile{\tau_n^\prime}^{\mathrm{pool},\star,\mathrm{bc}}
        (\bm\omega_\gamma,\bm\omega_q)}
         {\expectile{\tau_n^\prime}}
    -1
  \right)
  \;\todist\;
  \mathcal N\!\left(
    0,\;
    \bm\omega_\gamma^\top\Vc\bm\omega_\gamma
  \right).
\end{equation}
The same conclusion holds with estimated weights
$(\hat{\bm\omega}_\gamma,\hat{\bm\omega}_q)$ satisfying the exact
affine constraints
$\hat{\bm\omega}_\gamma^\top\bm 1
=\hat{\bm\omega}_q^\top\bm 1=1$ and
$\hat{\bm\omega}_\gamma\toprob\bm\omega_\gamma$,
$\hat{\bm\omega}_q\toprob\bm\omega_q$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
See \Cref{app:proofs:pool-ext-bias-reduced-clt}.
\end{proof}

\Cref{cor:pool-ext:bias-reduced-clt} is deliberately first-order:
$\Bc^\star$, $b^{\xi/\Quant}$ and $\bm\omega_q$ may still matter for
finite-sample or higher-order behaviour.

The practical cost is the same as in
\textcite{DaouiaPadoanStupfler2021}: estimating $\Bc$ usually requires
a second-order working model, such as a Hall-type form
$A(t)=\gamma\beta t^\rho$, and inherits the finite-sample instability of
second-order bias estimation
\parencite[Remark~4]{DaouiaPadoanStupfler2021};
see also the iid expectile discussion in
\textcite[\S3.1.1]{DaouiaGirardStupfler2018}.

After bias correction, the first-order objective is variance rather
than AMSE, so the natural companion weight is
$\bm\omega^{\mathrm{Var}}$ of \eqref{eq:pool-ext:var-opt}. The AMSE
weight remains valid but is not generally first-order variance-optimal.
No extra first-order variance term appears from $\hatBc$, since
$\bm\omega_\gamma^\top(\hatBc-\Bc)=o_p(1)$.

\section{Asymptotic confidence intervals}
\label{sec:pool-ext:CI}

The limiting theorem is used on the log scale, as in the pooled
Weissman-quantile and extreme-expectile intervals of
\textcite{DaouiaPadoanStupfler2021,DavisonPadoanStupfler2023}. Because
the intermediate anchor is first-order invisible, the standard error is
the pooled-Hill standard error multiplied by $\ell_n$.

For estimated Hill weights define
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:se-def}
  \hat\sigma_{\gamma,n}^{\star\,2}
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma}^{\top}\hatVc
  \hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma},
  \qquad
  \ell_n
  =
  \log\!\left(\frac{1-\tau_n}{1-\tau_n^\prime}\right).
\end{equation}
The anchor weight $\hat{\bm\omega}_q$ enters the point estimator but
not the first-order standard error.

\begin{corollary}[Variance-only confidence interval for
                  $\expectile{\tau_n^\prime}$]
\label{cor:pool-ext:CI}
Assume the setting of \Cref{thm:pool-ext:main}. Let
$(\hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma},\hat{\bm\omega}_{q})$ be estimated weights
satisfying the affine constraints
\[
  \hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma}^{\top}\bm 1
  =
  \hat{\bm\omega}_{q}^{\top}\bm 1
  =
  1
  \quad\text{exactly,}
  \qquad
  \hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma}\toprob\bm\omega_{\gamma},
  \qquad
  \hat{\bm\omega}_{q}\toprob\bm\omega_{q},
\]
for deterministic affine limits
$(\bm\omega_{\gamma},\bm\omega_{q})$. Assume also that
\[
  \hatVc \toprob \Vc,
  \qquad
  \bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\Vc\bm\omega_{\gamma}>0,
  \qquad
  \hat\sigma_{\gamma,n}^{\star\,2}>0
  \text{ with probability tending to one.}
\]
Let $\widehat\zeta_{\tau_n^\prime}$ denote either
\[
  \hatexpectile{\tau_n^\prime}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}
    (\hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma},\hat{\bm\omega}_{q})
\]
under the centring condition
$\bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\Bc=0$ --- in particular under the
undersmoothing calibration \eqref{eq:pool-int:undersmoothing} --- or
\[
  \hatexpectile{\tau_n^\prime}^{\mathrm{pool},\star,\mathrm{bc}}
    (\hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma},\hat{\bm\omega}_{q})
\]
under the bias-reduction assumptions of
\Cref{cor:pool-ext:bias-reduced-clt}. Under the positivity convention
of \Cref{sec:pool-ext:estimator} and the expectile--quantile
equivalence, $\widehat\zeta_{\tau_n^\prime}$ is positive with
probability tending to one and $\expectile{\tau_n^\prime}$ is positive
eventually. Then, for every $\alpha\in(0,1)$, with
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:CI-log}
  r_{n,\star}
  \;\coloneqq\;
  z_{1-\alpha/2}\,
  \ell_n
  \sqrt{\frac{\hat\sigma_{\gamma,n}^{\star\,2}}{k}},
  \qquad
  I_{n,\star}^{\log}
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \big[
    \log\widehat\zeta_{\tau_n^\prime}-r_{n,\star},\,
    \log\widehat\zeta_{\tau_n^\prime}+r_{n,\star}
  \big],
\end{equation}
the log-scale interval $I_{n,\star}^{\log}$ and its exponential image
\[
  I_{n,\star}
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \exp(I_{n,\star}^{\log})
  =
  [\exp(L_{n,-}),\exp(L_{n,+})]
  \quad\text{when } I_{n,\star}^{\log}=[L_{n,-},L_{n,+}],
\]
are asymptotic $(1-\alpha)$ confidence intervals for
$\log\expectile{\tau_n^\prime}$ and
$\expectile{\tau_n^\prime}$ respectively:
\[
  \Prob\!\big(\log\expectile{\tau_n^\prime}
        \in I_{n,\star}^{\log}\big)
  \;\to\;
  1-\alpha,
  \qquad
  \Prob\!\big(\expectile{\tau_n^\prime}
        \in I_{n,\star}\big)
  \;\to\;
  1-\alpha.
\]
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
See \Cref{app:proofs:pool-ext-CI}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Bias-centred interval]
\label{rem:pool-ext:CI-bias-centred}
If the asymptotic bias in \Cref{thm:pool-ext:main} is retained rather
than removed, the variance-only interval
\eqref{eq:pool-ext:CI-log} is not centred correctly unless
$\bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\Bc=0$. Suppose instead that
\[
  \hatBc \toprob \Bc,
  \qquad
  \hat\mu_{\gamma,n}^{\star}
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma}^{\top}\hatBc
  \toprob
  \bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\Bc .
\]
On the very-extreme scale this is the only surviving first-order bias.
With the same weights and $\hatBc$, centering is algebraically identical
to using \eqref{eq:pool-ext:bias-reduced-estimator}, since
\[
  \log
  \hatexpectile{\tau_n^\prime}^{\mathrm{pool},\star,\mathrm{bc}}
    (\hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma},\hat{\bm\omega}_{q})
  =
  \log
  \hatexpectile{\tau_n^\prime}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}
    (\hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma},\hat{\bm\omega}_{q})
  -
  \hat\mu_{\gamma,n}^{\star}\frac{\ell_n}{\sqrt{k}}.
\]
Then, for every $\alpha\in(0,1)$, using the radius $r_{n,\star}$ from
\eqref{eq:pool-ext:CI-log}, the log-scale bias-centred interval
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-ext:CI-log-bias-centred}
  C_{n,\star,\mathrm{bcen}}
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \log
  \hatexpectile{\tau_n^\prime}^{\mathrm{pool},\star}
    (\hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma},\hat{\bm\omega}_{q})
  -
  \hat\mu_{\gamma,n}^{\star}\frac{\ell_n}{\sqrt{k}},
  \qquad
  I_{n,\star,\mathrm{bcen}}^{\log}
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \big[
    C_{n,\star,\mathrm{bcen}}-r_{n,\star},\,
    C_{n,\star,\mathrm{bcen}}+r_{n,\star}
  \big]
\end{equation}
has the same asymptotic coverage as \eqref{eq:pool-ext:CI-log}, and so
does its exponential image. Feasibility again depends on consistent
Hill-bias estimation, usually via a second-order working model.
\end{remark}

The width of \eqref{eq:pool-ext:CI-log} is
\[
  2z_{1-\alpha/2}\,
  \ell_n
  \sqrt{\hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma}^{\top}\hatVc
        \hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma}/k}.
\]
Thus the width grows linearly with the extrapolation distance and
shrinks with the pooled Hill root-$k$ information. The standard error
does not involve $m(\gamma)$, $\bm L^\star$, $\Bc^\star$ or
$b^{\xi/\Quant}$, and there is no special restriction at
$m(\gamma)=0$.

In the distributed common-margin case, the variance-dominated default is
effective-sample-size Hill weighting
\eqref{eq:pool-ext:var-opt-distrib} with the matched convention
$\hat{\bm\omega}_q=\hat{\bm\omega}_\gamma$. Under undersmoothing, or
whenever $\bm\omega_\gamma^\top\Bc=0$, this gives the variance-only
interval without estimating $\Bc$. Otherwise one needs either
\eqref{eq:pool-ext:bias-reduced-estimator} or the bias-centred interval
\eqref{eq:pool-ext:CI-log-bias-centred}, both of which require
consistent Hill-bias estimation.
