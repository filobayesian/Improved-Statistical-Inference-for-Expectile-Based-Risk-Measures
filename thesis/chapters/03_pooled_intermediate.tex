\chapter[Withdrawn pooled-intermediate route]{Withdrawn pooled-intermediate expectile route}
\label{ch:pool-int}

\section{Status}
\label{sec:pool-int:status}

This chapter is intentionally no longer a contribution chapter. The
previous draft developed a standalone pooled intermediate
quantile-based expectile theory and used it as the foundation for the
very-extreme case. After supervisor feedback on 3 June 2026, that route
is withdrawn from the thesis architecture.

The following objects from the old draft must not be cited as settled
results:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item a standalone pooled intermediate expectile CLT obtained by
      applying the quantile-based expectile bridge at an intermediate
      level;
\item the comparison between pooling primitives first and pooling local
      quantile-based expectiles first;
\item the two-weight variance and AMSE formula for intermediate
      pooled expectiles;
\item closed-form variance- or AMSE-optimal expectile weights derived
      from that intermediate theory;
\item log-scale confidence intervals and the expectile-tail-homogeneity
      diagnostic based on those formulae.
\end{enumerate}

\section{What remains useful}
\label{sec:pool-int:useful-material}

Some ingredients that motivated the old chapter may still be useful as
background or scratch work, but they have to be reintroduced only after
the new estimator has been analysed from first principles. These
include the notation for pooled Hill estimators, weighted-geometric
Weissman estimators, the high-expectile constant $\psifn$, and
Taylor-expansion bookkeeping for
$\log\psifn(\widehat\gamma)-\log\psifn(\gamma)$.

Those ingredients belong either in the background chapter or in the new
main-theory chapter. They should not be used to preserve the old claim
that intermediate expectile pooling is the structural foundation of the
extreme-expectile result.

\section{Replacement role}
\label{sec:pool-int:replacement-role}

In the rebuilt thesis this chapter should eventually be deleted,
shortened into a source-audit note, or replaced by the new main theory.
For now it serves one purpose: to mark the obsolete route clearly so
the remaining draft does not accidentally present withdrawn derivations
as validated thesis contributions.
