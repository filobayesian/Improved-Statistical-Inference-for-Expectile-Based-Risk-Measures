\chapter{Pooled inference for extreme expectiles at intermediate levels}
\label{ch:pool-int}

\section*{Overview}

This chapter introduces the pooled extreme-expectile estimator at
intermediate levels and develops its asymptotic theory. The construction
plugs the pooled tail-index estimator $\hat\gamma_n(\bm\omega_\gamma)$
and the pooled weighted-geometric Weissman extreme-quantile estimator
$\hatWei_n(\,\cdot\,;\bm\omega_q)$ of
\textcite{DaouiaPadoanStupfler2021} into the expectile--quantile
asymptotic equivalence $\xi_\tau \approx \psifn(\gamma)\,Q_X(\tau)$. The
delta method applied through $\psifn$ yields a joint Gaussian limit,
from which we read off the variance- and AMSE-optimal pooling weights
tailored to the pooled expectile target.

\section{Setup and notation}
\label{sec:pool-int:setup}

The contribution of this chapter rests on the pooling framework of
\Cref{sec:bg:pooling}, specialised to independent samples with a
common marginal distribution --- the tail-homogeneous
distributed-inference case of \Cref{sec:bg:pool-distrib}. The equal
effective sample fraction condition $k_{j}/n_{j} = (k/n)(1 + o(1))$
of \Cref{cor:bg:pool-distrib-oracle} is \emph{not} part of the
standing setup; it is invoked only in
\Cref{sec:pool-int:weights,sec:pool-int:CI} where it simplifies the
variance-optimal weights. We restate below only those assumptions
used throughout; the general pooling machinery and its supporting
joint central limit theorems
(\Cref{thm:bg:pool-hill,thm:bg:pool-weissman}) are imported without
modification.

\paragraph{The $m$ samples and the pooling primitives.}
We have $m$ \emph{independent} samples
$\{X_{i, j}\}_{i = 1, \ldots, n_{j}}$, $j = 1, \ldots, m$, with common
marginal distribution function $F$ and common extreme-value index
$\gamma$. The number of samples $m$ is fixed as $n \to \infty$
throughout the chapter; all CLTs below are finite-$m$ statements. Independence of the samples implies asymptotic tail
independence, hence the pairwise tail-copula functions of
\Cref{cond:bg:tail-copula} vanish:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:R-zero}
  \Rjl \;\equiv\; 0
  \qquad\text{for every } j \ne \ell.
\end{equation}
The proportionality conditions \eqref{eq:bg:pool-rates} are in force,
with $k_{j} \to \infty$ and $k_{j}/n_{j} \to 0$, and the total
effective sample size is $k = \sum_{j = 1}^{m} k_{j}$. Each sample
$j$ supplies the two pooling primitives of \Cref{sec:bg:pooling}: a
local Hill estimator $\hat\gamma_{j}(k_{j})$ as in
\eqref{eq:bg:hill}, and a local Weissman extreme-quantile estimator
$\hat q^{\star}_{j}(\,\cdot\,\mid k_{j})$ as in
\eqref{eq:bg:weissman}. These $2m$ scalars, together with their
joint asymptotic covariance, constitute the entire information
shared between samples; no raw observation and no expectile-specific
summary statistic crosses between machines.

\paragraph{Heavy-tail regime.}
The common marginal $F$ satisfies the second-order condition
$\mathcal C_{2}(\gamma, \rho, A)$ of \Cref{cond:bg:second-order} with
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:moment}
  \gamma \in (0, 1/2),
  \qquad
  \E\big|\min(X, 0)\big|^{2 + \delta} < \infty
  \quad\text{for some } \delta > 0,
\end{equation}
and the per-sample bias-rate condition
$\sqrt{k_{j}}\, A(n_{j}/k_{j}) \to \lambda_{j} \in \R$ holds for every
$j$. The restriction $\gamma < 1/2$ is the moment regime in which
expectile inference is statistically meaningful and in which the
expectile--quantile asymptotic equivalence
\eqref{eq:bg:expectile-quantile-equivalence} composes with the iid
intermediate central limit theorems
\Cref{thm:bg:laws-iid,thm:bg:qb-iid}.

\paragraph{The target.}
We target the common population expectile $\expectile{\tau_{n}}$ at
an \emph{intermediate} level $\tau_{n} \to 1$ with
$n(1 - \tau_{n}) \to \infty$, in the regime sense of
\Cref{sec:bg:extreme-expectile}. In addition, the per-sample
log-rate factor
\[
  L_{j}(\tau_{n})
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \log\!\frac{k_{j}}{n_{j}(1 - \tau_{n})}
\]
is assumed to admit a finite limit $L_{j}^{\star} \in \R$ for every
$j$; equivalently, $1 - \tau_{n}$ has the same order as $k_{j}/n_{j}$
for each sample. This condition controls the Hill--Weissman
cross-covariance in \Cref{prop:pool-int:joint-clt} and is the
chapter's link between the chosen target level and the local
effective sample fractions. The very-extreme regime
$n(1 - \tau_{n}^{\prime}) \to c \in [0, \infty)$ is deferred to
\Cref{ch:pool-ext}, where the pooled intermediate-level result proved
here serves as the anchor for Weissman-style extrapolation through
\Cref{prop:bg:plug-in-clt}.

\paragraph{Per-sample QB plug-in: a forced consequence of the pooling protocol.}
Because each sample shares the pooling primitives
$(\hat\gamma_{j}(k_{j}),\,\hat q^{\star}_{j}(\,\cdot\,\mid k_{j}))$
and no expectile-specific summary statistic, the only expectile
estimator that the chapter can construct \emph{locally} on each
sample, before pooling, is the QB plug-in
\eqref{eq:bg:qb} obtained by factoring through the
expectile--quantile equivalence of \Cref{prop:bg:expectile-quantile}:
\[
  \hatexpectile{\tau,\,j}
  \;=\;
  \psifn\!\big(\hat\gamma_{j}(k_{j})\big)\,
  \hat q^{\star}_{j}(1 - \tau \mid k_{j}).
\]
The LAWS direct estimator \eqref{eq:bg:laws} would require sharing
$\tildexpectile{\tau_{n},\,j}$ across samples, which lies outside the
pooling protocol of \Cref{sec:bg:pooling}. The comparison of
\Cref{thm:bg:qb-iid,thm:bg:laws-iid} shows that neither LAWS nor QB
uniformly dominates the other over $\gamma \in (0, 1/2)$; the QB
construction is therefore adopted not because it is uniformly more
efficient, but because it is the only intermediate-expectile
estimator that can be assembled from the communicated pooling
primitives. The choice is a derived consequence of the pooling
protocol, not an independent methodological preference.

\paragraph{Roadmap.}
\Cref{sec:pool-int:estimator} defines the pooled QB intermediate
expectile estimator from the pooled Hill and pooled Weissman inputs.
\Cref{sec:pool-int:joint-clt} derives the joint $\sqrt{k}$-CLT for
the pooled Hill and log-Weissman inputs, combining the marginal
pooled limits of \Cref{sec:bg:pooling}
(\Cref{thm:bg:pool-hill,thm:bg:pool-weissman}) with the local
Hill / intermediate-threshold joint CLT, specialised to the
independent-samples case \eqref{eq:pool-int:R-zero}. The output is
the Gaussian input to which the delta method is then applied.
\Cref{sec:pool-int:expectile-clt} applies the delta method through
$\psifn$ and states the main asymptotic-normality theorem of the
chapter. \Cref{sec:pool-int:weights} derives the variance- and
AMSE-optimal pooling weights for the expectile target and compares
them with the corresponding optima for the quantile target.
\Cref{sec:pool-int:CI} provides feasible confidence intervals built
from estimated weights, and \Cref{sec:pool-int:test} adapts the
tail-homogeneity test of \Cref{thm:bg:tail-homogeneity-test} to the
expectile-tail-homogeneity null hypothesis.

\section{The pooled QB expectile estimator}
\label{sec:pool-int:estimator}

The shared primitives $\big(\hat\gamma_{j}(k_{j}),\,
\hat q^{\star}_{j}(1 - \tau_{n} \mid k_{j})\big)_{j = 1, \ldots, m}$
admit two natural \emph{single-weight} constructions of a pooled QB
intermediate-expectile estimator. The first, denoted (A), applies
the QB plug-in once, at the centre, after pooling the Hill and
Weissman primitives separately; the second, denoted (B), computes a
per-sample QB expectile on each machine and then geometric-pools
the $m$ local estimates. At matched single weights --- the only
setting in which the two constructions are directly comparable ---
\Cref{prop:pool-int:A-B-equiv} below shows they share the same
$\sqrt{k}$-asymptotic distribution, so on this common domain the
choice between them is asymptotically inert. We adopt (A) by
parsimony, since it is the direct lift of the iid QB pipeline of
\Cref{thm:bg:qb-iid} --- one tail-index input, one quantile input,
one application of $\psifn$ --- to the pooled setting.

Crucially, (A) admits an intrinsic \emph{two-weight} extension that
(B) does not: the pooled Hill and pooled Weissman primitives can in
principle receive distinct weight vectors $(\bm\omega_{\gamma},
\bm\omega_{q})$. Once we relax to two weights, construction (B) has
no analogue --- it is built on a single per-sample QB and a single
geometric pool --- so the two-weight machinery developed in the
remainder of the chapter is necessarily (A)-only.
\Cref{sec:pool-int:weights} will return to the question of whether
this two-weight freedom is ever genuinely exploited, and show that
under the distributed-inference setting of \Cref{sec:pool-int:setup}
the variance-optimal pair lies on the matched-weight diagonal.

\paragraph{Pooled Hill and pooled Weissman primitives.}
For weight vectors $\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q} \in \R^{m}$
satisfying $\bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\bm 1
= \bm\omega_{q}^{\top}\bm 1 = 1$, the linear pooled Hill estimator
\eqref{eq:bg:pooled-hill} and the weighted-geometric pooled
Weissman estimator \eqref{eq:bg:pooled-weissman} read
\begin{align}
\label{eq:pool-int:hill-pool}
  \hat\gamma_{n}(\bm\omega_{\gamma})
    &\;=\;
    \bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\hat{\bm\gamma}_{n}
    \;=\;
    \sum_{j=1}^{m}
      \omega_{\gamma, j}\,\hat\gamma_{j}(k_{j}),
  \\[2pt]
\label{eq:pool-int:weissman-pool}
  \hatWei_{n}(1 - \tau_{n} \mid \bm\omega_{q})
    &\;=\;
    \prod_{j=1}^{m}
      \big[\hat q^{\star}_{j}(1 - \tau_{n} \mid k_{j})\big]^{\omega_{q, j}},
\end{align}
both evaluated at the chapter's intermediate level $\tau_{n}$.

\paragraph{The two candidate estimators.}
The first construction, which we adopt as canonical, pools the
primitives separately and applies the QB plug-in once at the
centre,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:expectile-A}
  \hatexpectile{\tau_{n}}^{(A)}\!
    (\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q})
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \psifn\!\big(\hat\gamma_{n}(\bm\omega_{\gamma})\big)\,
  \hatWei_{n}(1 - \tau_{n} \mid \bm\omega_{q}).
\end{equation}
The second computes a per-sample QB expectile on each machine and
geometric-pools the resulting $m$ local estimates,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:expectile-B}
  \hatexpectile{\tau_{n}}^{(B)}\!(\bm\omega)
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \prod_{j = 1}^{m}\!\big[
    \psifn(\hat\gamma_{j}(k_{j}))\,
    \hat q^{\star}_{j}(1 - \tau_{n} \mid k_{j})
  \big]^{\omega_{j}}
  \;=\;
  \Big(\prod_{j = 1}^{m}\psifn(\hat\gamma_{j}(k_{j}))^{\omega_{j}}\Big)
  \cdot \hatWei_{n}(1 - \tau_{n} \mid \bm\omega).
\end{equation}
The Weissman factor is identical in the two formulae; the
constructions differ only in whether the nonlinearity $\psifn$ acts
once on the pooled $\hat\gamma_{n}$ (construction (A)) or
componentwise on the $m$ per-sample $\hat\gamma_{j}$'s followed by
weighted-geometric averaging (construction (B)). The iid QB
plug-in of \Cref{thm:bg:qb-iid} ($m = 1$) reduces both formulae
to $\psifn(\hat\gamma)\,\hat q^{\star}$; construction (A) is the
direct $m \ge 2$ generalisation of that structure, whereas (B)
introduces $m$ independent applications of $\psifn$ that have no
counterpart in the iid baseline.

\begin{proposition}[$\sqrt{k}$-asymptotic equivalence of (A) and (B)]
\label{prop:pool-int:A-B-equiv}
Under the assumptions of \Cref{sec:pool-int:setup}, for fixed
$m \in \mathbb{N}$ and every deterministic weight vector
$\bm\omega \in \R^{m}$ with $\bm\omega^{\top}\bm 1 = 1$,
\[
  \sqrt{k}\,
  \log\!\frac{\hatexpectile{\tau_{n}}^{(A)}(\bm\omega, \bm\omega)}
              {\hatexpectile{\tau_{n}}^{(B)}(\bm\omega)}
  \;\toprob\; 0.
\]
In fact the unscaled log-ratio is itself $O_{p}(k^{-1})$, which is
strictly stronger than the $o_{p}(k^{-1/2})$ statement required by
the $\sqrt{k}$-CLT below. Consequently, when construction (A) is
evaluated at matched weights
$(\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q}) = (\bm\omega, \bm\omega)$, the
two estimators share the same $\sqrt{k}$-asymptotic distribution and
\Cref{thm:pool-int:main} applies to either without modification. The
same conclusion extends to estimated weights $\hat{\bm\omega}$
satisfying $\hat{\bm\omega} \toprob \bm\omega$: since $\bm\omega$ is
a fixed finite vector, the components of $\hat{\bm\omega}$ are
automatically $O_{p}(1)$ (cf.\ \Cref{cor:pool-int:CI}).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Sketch.]
A first-order Taylor expansion of $\log\psifn$ around $\gamma$
gives $\log\psifn(\hat\gamma_{j}) - \log\psifn(\gamma) = m(\gamma)
(\hat\gamma_{j} - \gamma) + O_{p}((\hat\gamma_{j} - \gamma)^{2})$,
and analogously for $\hat\gamma_{n}(\bm\omega)$. The linear terms
in $\log\hatexpectile{}^{(A)}$ and $\log\hatexpectile{}^{(B)}$
coincide because
$\sum_{j} \omega_{j}(\hat\gamma_{j} - \gamma) = \hat\gamma_{n}
(\bm\omega) - \gamma$, so the discrepancy is the difference of
quadratic-and-higher remainders, which is $O_{p}(1/k)$. On the
$\sqrt{k}$-scale this is $O_{p}(k^{-1/2}) = o_{p}(1)$. Full proof
in \Cref{app:proofs:A-B-equiv}.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Canonical choice.}
We adopt
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:expectile-pool}
  \hatexpectile{\tau_{n}}^{\mathrm{pool}}\!
    (\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q})
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \psifn\!\big(\hat\gamma_{n}(\bm\omega_{\gamma})\big)\,
  \hatWei_{n}(1 - \tau_{n} \mid \bm\omega_{q}),
\end{equation}
identical to \eqref{eq:pool-int:expectile-A}, as the canonical
pooled QB intermediate-expectile estimator for the remainder of
this chapter. The choice is justified by parsimony:
\eqref{eq:pool-int:expectile-A} reproduces the iid QB pipeline of
\Cref{thm:bg:qb-iid} --- one tail-index input, one quantile input,
one application of $\psifn$ --- directly at the pooled level,
whereas \eqref{eq:pool-int:expectile-B} introduces $m$ applications
of $\psifn$ which, by \Cref{prop:pool-int:A-B-equiv}, contribute
no additional asymptotic information.\footnote{At the second order,
the two constructions differ through a Jensen-type term of order
$k^{-1}$ involving $m'(\gamma)$ and the dispersion of the local Hill
estimators across samples; its sign depends on the weight vector
and need not favour either construction in general. This term sits
below the order of the $\sqrt{k}$-asymptotic theory developed here
and below the standard EVI bias control
$\sqrt{k_{j}}\,A(n_{j}/k_{j}) \to \lambda_{j}$ of
\Cref{thm:bg:hill}, so we do not use it.}

\paragraph{Two weight vectors, not one.}
Definition \eqref{eq:pool-int:expectile-pool} carries two free weight
vectors rather than a single one. In the quantile-only analysis of
\Cref{thm:bg:pool-weissman} the joint asymptotic distribution of the
pooled Hill and pooled Weissman estimators is governed by a single
$\bm\omega$, and the variance- and AMSE-optima of
\Cref{prop:bg:optimal-weights} apply uniformly across the two
primitives. The expectile target combines these two primitives
through the nonlinear map $\psifn$, and the asymptotic variance of
$\hatexpectile{\tau_{n}}^{\mathrm{pool}}$ acquires a contribution
from the within-sample cross-covariance of $\hat\gamma_{j}$ and
$\hat q^{\star}_{j}$ that is absent in the quantile-only analysis.
This contribution can in principle pull the variance-optimal
$\bm\omega_{\gamma}$ apart from the variance-optimal
$\bm\omega_{q}$; \Cref{sec:pool-int:weights} identifies the exact
discrepancy and the sufficient conditions under which the two
optima coincide. Keeping the weights separate in the definition
leaves that section free to specify their relationship rather than
imposing it by hand.

\paragraph{Why the pooled Weissman at the intermediate level.}
At the intermediate level $\tau_{n}$, the per-sample Weissman
estimator $\hat q^{\star}_{j}(1 - \tau_{n} \mid k_{j}) = (k_{j}/(n_{j}
(1 - \tau_{n})))^{\hat\gamma_{j}(k_{j})}\, X_{n_{j} - k_{j} : n_{j},\,j}$
plays a dual role. When $\tau_{n}$ matches the sample-specific
intermediate level $\tau_{n_{j}} \coloneqq 1 - k_{j}/n_{j}$, the
multiplicative power factor collapses to one and
$\hat q^{\star}_{j}$ reduces to the bare $(k_{j} + 1)$-th upper order
statistic of sample $j$ --- the empirical intermediate quantile.
Otherwise, $\hat q^{\star}_{j}$ interpolates or mildly extrapolates
across intermediate levels, and remains $\sqrt{k_{j}}$-consistent by
the within-sample joint CLT for the Hill estimator and the
intermediate log-order statistic invoked in
\Cref{prop:pool-int:joint-clt} below --- this is the finite-log-rate
analogue of the diverging-log-rate Weissman CLT
\Cref{thm:bg:weissman}, not a direct consequence of it. Adopting the Weissman primitive
uniformly --- in lieu of switching to bare order statistics at
intermediate levels --- has two virtues: (i) it is the primitive that
the pooling protocol of \Cref{sec:bg:pooling} already shares between
samples, so no additional summary statistic is required; (ii) it
carries the same form to the extreme regime treated in
\Cref{ch:pool-ext}, keeping the two chapters on a single notational
footing.

\section{Joint asymptotic distribution of the pooled inputs}
\label{sec:pool-int:joint-clt}

This section establishes the joint $\sqrt{k}$-Gaussian limit of
$(\hat\gamma_{n}(\bm\omega_{\gamma}),\,
\log\hatWei_{n}(1 - \tau_{n} \mid \bm\omega_{q}))$ at intermediate
level $\tau_{n}$. The result is the input that
\Cref{sec:pool-int:expectile-clt} feeds through the delta method to
obtain the asymptotic distribution of the pooled QB expectile
estimator \eqref{eq:pool-int:expectile-pool}.

\paragraph{Within-sample decomposition.}
For each sample $j$, the log of the Weissman estimator decomposes
exactly as
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:weissman-decomp}
  \log\hat q^{\star}_{j}(1 - \tau_{n} \mid k_{j})
  \;=\;
  \log X_{n_{j} - k_{j} : n_{j},\,j}
  \;+\;
  L_{j}(\tau_{n})\, \hat\gamma_{j}(k_{j}),
  \qquad
  L_{j}(\tau_{n}) \coloneqq
    \log\!\frac{k_{j}}{n_{j}(1 - \tau_{n})}.
\end{equation}
The log-rate factor $L_{j}(\tau_{n})$ records the extent to which
sample $j$ extrapolates from its own intermediate level
$\tau_{n_{j}} \coloneqq 1 - k_{j}/n_{j}$ to the chapter target
$\tau_{n}$: $L_{j} = 0$ when the two coincide, $L_{j} > 0$ for
extrapolation, $L_{j} < 0$ for interpolation. The only non-algebraic input
to the entire proof of \Cref{prop:pool-int:joint-clt} is the
within-sample joint asymptotic behaviour of the Hill estimator and
its companion intermediate log-order statistic at effective sample
size $k_{j}$. By the tail-empirical-process representation
\parencite[Theorem~2.4.8 and \S5.1]{deHaanFerreira2006}, the two
statistics are jointly Gaussian on the $\sqrt{k_{j}}$-scale,
\emph{asymptotically independent}, and each has asymptotic variance
$\gamma^{2}$. The zero off-diagonal entry is structural: the Hill
estimator is a smooth functional of the centred log-spacings above
$X_{n_{j} - k_{j} : n_{j},\,j}$, while the intermediate-threshold
fluctuation is the centred $(k_{j}+1)$-th upper log-order statistic
itself, and the two decouple in the tail-empirical-process limit.
This same joint behaviour underlies the iid QB plug-in CLT of
\textcite{DaouiaGirardStupfler2018} reproduced in
\Cref{thm:bg:qb-iid}. Combining it with
\eqref{eq:pool-int:weissman-decomp} yields the within-sample joint
central limit theorem
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:per-sample-clt}
  \sqrt{k_{j}}\!
    \begin{pmatrix}
      \hat\gamma_{j}(k_{j}) - \gamma \\[2pt]
      \log\hat q^{\star}_{j}(1 - \tau_{n} \mid k_{j})
        - \log \Quant_{X}(\tau_{n})
    \end{pmatrix}
  \;\todist\;
  \mathcal N\!\left(\bm\mu_{j}(\tau_{n}),\,
                    \gamma^{2}\!\begin{pmatrix}
                      1 & L_{j}^{\star} \\
                      L_{j}^{\star} & 1 + (L_{j}^{\star})^{2}
                    \end{pmatrix}\right),
\end{equation}
under the common-marginal assumption of \Cref{sec:pool-int:setup}
and the condition $L_{j}(\tau_{n}) \to L_{j}^{\star} \in \R$. The
bias vector $\bm\mu_{j}(\tau_{n})$ is determined by the second-order
condition $\mathcal C_{2}(\gamma, \rho, A)$; its explicit form is
deferred to the proof appendix. The $2 \times 2$ covariance reveals
the within-sample Hill--Weissman correlation induced by the log-rate
factor: cross-covariance $\gamma^{2} L_{j}^{\star}$, and a Weissman
variance $\gamma^{2}(1 + (L_{j}^{\star})^{2})$ obtained from the
asymptotically independent order-statistic and rescaled-Hill
contributions.

\paragraph{Pooling under independence.}
Independence of the $m$ samples implies that the cross-sample
covariance blocks of the $2m$-vector
$\big(\hat\gamma_{j} - \gamma,\, \log\hat q^{\star}_{j} - \log
\Quant_{X}(\tau_{n})\big)_{j = 1, \ldots, m}$ vanish identically.
Linear pooling with weight vectors
$(\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q})$ then collapses this
$2m$-vector onto a $2$-vector whose covariance is assembled
block-by-block from the within-sample blocks of
\eqref{eq:pool-int:per-sample-clt}, each rescaled to the common
$\sqrt{k}$-scale via the proportionality conditions
\eqref{eq:bg:pool-rates}.

\begin{proposition}[Joint pooled CLT at intermediate level]
\label{prop:pool-int:joint-clt}
Under the assumptions of \Cref{sec:pool-int:setup} and the
convergence $L_{j}(\tau_{n}) \to L_{j}^{\star} \in \R$ for every $j$,
for any weight vectors $\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q} \in \R^{m}$
satisfying
$\bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\bm 1 = \bm\omega_{q}^{\top}\bm 1 = 1$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:joint-pooled-clt}
  \sqrt{k}\!
    \begin{pmatrix}
      \hat\gamma_{n}(\bm\omega_{\gamma}) - \gamma \\[2pt]
      \log\hatWei_{n}(1 - \tau_{n} \mid \bm\omega_{q})
        - \log \Quant_{X}(\tau_{n})
    \end{pmatrix}
  \;\todist\;
  \mathcal N\!\big(\bm\mu(\bm\omega_{\gamma},\bm\omega_{q}),\,
                    \bm\Sigma(\bm\omega_{\gamma},\bm\omega_{q})\big),
\end{equation}
with covariance
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:Sigma}
  \bm\Sigma(\bm\omega_{\gamma},\bm\omega_{q})
  \;=\;
  \begin{pmatrix}
    \bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\Vc\bm\omega_{\gamma}
      &
    \bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\,\mathrm{diag}(\bm L^{\star})\,
      \Vc\,\bm\omega_{q} \\[2pt]
    \bm\omega_{q}^{\top}\,\mathrm{diag}(\bm L^{\star})\,
      \Vc\,\bm\omega_{\gamma}
      &
    \bm\omega_{q}^{\top}\!\big(\Vc + \mathrm{diag}(\bm L^{\star})^{2}
      \Vc\big)\,\bm\omega_{q}
  \end{pmatrix},
\end{equation}
where $\Vc$ is the diagonal matrix \eqref{eq:bg:V_c-diagonal},
$\bm L^{\star} = (L_{1}^{\star}, \ldots, L_{m}^{\star})^{\top}$, and
mean
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:mu-pool}
  \bm\mu(\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q})
  \;=\;
  \begin{pmatrix}
    \bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\Bc \\[2pt]
    \bm\omega_{q}^{\top}\Bc^{\star}
  \end{pmatrix},
\end{equation}
where $\Bc$ is the pooled-Hill bias vector of \Cref{thm:bg:pool-hill}
and the limiting pooled-Weissman bias vector at the intermediate
level has entries
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:Bcstar-def}
  \big(\Bc^{\star}\big)_{j}
  \;=\;
  \sqrt{c_{j}\textstyle\sum_{i = 1}^{m} c_{i}^{-1}}\,
  \Big[L_{j}^{\star}\,b_{j}^{H}
       \;+\; b_{j}^{Q}
       \;-\; \lambda_{j}\,\Psi_{\rho}(e^{L_{j}^{\star}})\Big],
\end{equation}
with $b_{j}^{H} = \lambda_{j}/(1-\rho)$, $b_{j}^{Q}$ the second-order
bias of the intermediate log-order statistic (determined by
\Cref{cond:bg:second-order}), and $\Psi_{\rho}(y) = (y^{\rho}-1)/\rho$.
The explicit expression of $b_{j}^{Q}$ is not used below; only its
finiteness under $\mathcal C_{2}(\gamma, \rho, A)$ is required, and
the covariance \eqref{eq:pool-int:Sigma} does not depend on it.
The proposition is stated for deterministic weights; the extension to
estimated weights $\hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma}, \hat{\bm\omega}_{q}
\toprob \bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q}$ is a Slutsky corollary,
deferred to \Cref{sec:pool-int:CI}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
See \Cref{app:proofs:joint-clt}.
\end{proof}

\Cref{prop:pool-int:joint-clt} is the joint counterpart, at the
intermediate finite-log-rate level $L_{j}(\tau_{n}) \to L_{j}^{\star}
\in \R$, of the marginal pooled Hill and pooled Weissman limits
recalled in \Cref{sec:bg:pooling}
(\Cref{thm:bg:pool-hill,thm:bg:pool-weissman}). The extreme-level,
diverging-log-rate joint analogue is the subject of
\Cref{ch:pool-ext}. The
new feature for the expectile target is the cross-covariance
$\bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\mathrm{diag}(\bm L^{\star})\Vc\bm\omega_{q}$:
it is the structural reason why the variance-optimal weights
$(\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q})$ for the pooled expectile
estimator need not coincide, an observation that
\Cref{sec:pool-int:weights} develops quantitatively.

\section{Asymptotic normality of the pooled expectile estimator}
\label{sec:pool-int:expectile-clt}

The main result of this chapter is the central limit theorem for the
pooled QB intermediate expectile estimator
\eqref{eq:pool-int:expectile-pool}. The result is obtained by feeding
the joint pooled CLT of \Cref{prop:pool-int:joint-clt} through the
smooth log-linear map $(g, x) \mapsto \log\psifn(g) + x$ via a
first-order Taylor expansion, combined with the second-order
expectile--quantile expansion that controls the deterministic gap
between $\log\expectile{\tau_{n}}$ and $\log\psifn(\gamma) +
\log\Quant_{X}(\tau_{n})$.

\paragraph{Derivative of $\log \psifn$.}
A short calculation gives
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:m-function}
  \frac{d}{dg} \log \psifn(g)
  \;=\;
  \frac{1}{1 - g} - \log(g^{-1} - 1)
  \;=:\;
  m(g),
\end{equation}
which is the same $m(\gamma)$ that governs the variance of the QB
intermediate-level estimator in the iid baseline
\Cref{thm:bg:qb-iid}. The function $m(\,\cdot\,)$ is continuous on
$(0, 1)$, vanishes at the value $\gamma^{\star} \approx 0.2178$
solving $(1 - \gamma)^{-1} = \log(\gamma^{-1} - 1)$, and is positive
(resp.\ negative) for $\gamma$ above (resp.\ below) $\gamma^{\star}$.

\paragraph{The deterministic expectile--quantile gap.}
The first-order asymptotic equivalence
$\expectile{\tau} / \Quant_{X}(\tau) \to \psifn(\gamma)$ of
\Cref{prop:bg:expectile-quantile} sharpens, under
\Cref{cond:bg:second-order}, to a second-order expansion
\parencite[Lemma~1 and Theorem~2]{DaouiaGirardStupfler2018}
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:xi-Q-gap}
  \log\expectile{\tau} \;-\; \log\psifn(\gamma) \;-\; \log\Quant_{X}(\tau)
  \;=\;
  c(\gamma, \rho)\, A\!\big((1 - \tau)^{-1}\big)
  \;+\; o\!\big(A((1 - \tau)^{-1})\big),
  \qquad \tau \uparrow 1,
\end{equation}
for an explicit constant $c(\gamma, \rho)$ whose form is recorded in
the proof appendix and is not used below. The chapter setup of
\Cref{sec:pool-int:setup} already \emph{implies} a finite limit
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:Lambda-bullet}
  \Lambda^{\bullet}
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \lim_{n \to \infty}\sqrt{k}\,A\!\big((1 - \tau_{n})^{-1}\big)
  \;\in\; \R,
\end{equation}
without any additional hypothesis: starting from the per-sample bias
rate $\sqrt{k_{j}}\,A(n_{j}/k_{j}) \to \lambda_{j}$, the log-rate
convergence $L_{j}(\tau_{n}) \to L_{j}^{\star}$, and the regular
variation $|A| \in \mathrm{RV}_{\rho}$ at infinity
\parencite[Theorem~2.3.9]{deHaanFerreira2006}, one obtains
$\sqrt{k_{j}}\,A((1 - \tau_{n})^{-1}) \to \lambda_{j}\,
(e^{L_{j}^{\star}})^{\rho}$, and a further rescaling by
$\sqrt{k/k_{j}} \to \sqrt{c_{j}\sum_{i} c_{i}^{-1}}$ delivers
\eqref{eq:pool-int:Lambda-bullet} (the per-$j$ expressions for
$\Lambda^{\bullet}$ agree by joint compatibility of
$\{\lambda_{j}\}, \{L_{j}^{\star}\}, \{c_{j}\}$ under a single global
$A$; see \Cref{app:proofs:main-clt} for the explicit verification).
The finite asymptotic constant
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:b-xi-Q-def}
  b^{\xi/\Quant}
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \lim_{n \to \infty}\sqrt{k}\,
  \big[\log\psifn(\gamma) + \log\Quant_{X}(\tau_{n})
       - \log\expectile{\tau_{n}}\big]
  \;=\;
  -\,c(\gamma, \rho)\,\Lambda^{\bullet}
\end{equation}
follows. The sign convention in \eqref{eq:pool-int:b-xi-Q-def} is
chosen so that $b^{\xi/\Quant}$ enters the asymptotic mean of the
pooled estimator with a $+$ sign in \eqref{eq:pool-int:mu-xi} below;
that is, $b^{\xi/\Quant}$ is the deterministic \emph{bias
contribution} of the gap, not the gap itself. The contribution
$b^{\xi/\Quant}$ is \emph{absent} from the analogue quantile-target
theorem \Cref{thm:bg:pool-weissman} of
\textcite{DaouiaPadoanStupfler2021}: it is the deterministic
correction that arises when the quantile target is replaced by the
expectile target through the QB plug-in.

\begin{theorem}[Pooled QB intermediate expectile CLT]
\label{thm:pool-int:main}
Under the assumptions of \Cref{prop:pool-int:joint-clt} (in
particular \Cref{cond:bg:second-order}), for any weight vectors
$\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q} \in \R^{m}$ satisfying
$\bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\bm 1 = \bm\omega_{q}^{\top}\bm 1 = 1$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:main-clt}
  \sqrt{k}\!\left(
    \frac{\hatexpectile{\tau_{n}}^{\mathrm{pool}}\!
          (\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q})}
         {\expectile{\tau_{n}}}
    - 1\right)
  \;\todist\;
  \mathcal N\!\big(\mu^{\xi}(\bm\omega_{\gamma},\bm\omega_{q}),\,
                    \sigma^{\xi}(\bm\omega_{\gamma},\bm\omega_{q})^{2}\big),
\end{equation}
with asymptotic variance
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:sigma-xi}
  \sigma^{\xi}(\bm\omega_{\gamma},\bm\omega_{q})^{2}
  \;=\;
  m(\gamma)^{2}\,\bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\Vc\bm\omega_{\gamma}
  \;+\;
  2\, m(\gamma)\,
    \bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\mathrm{diag}(\bm L^{\star})\Vc\bm\omega_{q}
  \;+\;
  \bm\omega_{q}^{\top}\!\big(\Vc + \mathrm{diag}(\bm L^{\star})^{2}
    \Vc\big)\bm\omega_{q},
\end{equation}
and asymptotic bias
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:mu-xi}
  \mu^{\xi}(\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q})
  \;=\;
  m(\gamma)\,\bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\Bc
  \;+\;
  \bm\omega_{q}^{\top}\Bc^{\star}
  \;+\;
  b^{\xi/\Quant},
\end{equation}
where $\Bc$ and $\Bc^{\star}$ are as in
\Cref{prop:pool-int:joint-clt} (the pooled-Hill and limiting
intermediate-level pooled-Weissman bias vectors) and $b^{\xi/\Quant}$
is the asymptotic expectile--quantile bias contribution of
\eqref{eq:pool-int:b-xi-Q-def}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
See \Cref{app:proofs:main-clt}.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Reduction to the iid baseline.}
\Cref{thm:pool-int:main} contains \Cref{thm:bg:qb-iid} as the
special case $m = 1$ with the chapter target aligned to the single
sample's intermediate level. The weight vectors collapse to scalars
$\omega_{\gamma} = \omega_{q} = 1$; the alignment forces
$L_{1}^{\star} = 0$, $\Psi_{\rho}(e^{L_{1}^{\star}}) = 0$, and
$\Vc \to \gamma^{2}$. The asymptotic variance \eqref{eq:pool-int:sigma-xi}
collapses to $\gamma^{2}(1 + m(\gamma)^{2})$, in exact agreement with
\Cref{thm:bg:qb-iid}. The bias \eqref{eq:pool-int:mu-xi} collapses to
$m(\gamma)\,b_{1}^{H} + b_{1}^{Q} + b^{\xi/\Quant}
= m(\gamma)\,\lambda / (1 - \rho) + [b_{1}^{Q} + b^{\xi/\Quant}]$,
and the structural cancellation
$b_{1}^{Q} + b^{\xi/\Quant} = 0$ in the iid baseline --- a
known consequence of the joint asymptotic theory of the bare
intermediate-order statistic and the expectile--quantile equivalence
under $\mathcal C_{2}(\gamma, \rho, A)$, see
\textcite[Theorem~2]{DaouiaGirardStupfler2018} --- recovers the bias
$m(\gamma)\,\lambda / (1 - \rho)$ of \Cref{thm:bg:qb-iid}.

\paragraph{Distributed-inference simplification.}
Under the distributed-inference assumption that the sample fractions
are asymptotically equal, $k_{j}/n_{j} = (k/n)(1 + o(1))$ for every
$j$ (\Cref{cor:bg:pool-distrib-oracle}), the log-rate factor
$L_{j}(\tau_{n}) = \log(k/(n(1 - \tau_{n}))) + o(1)$ becomes a
sample-free quantity. Writing $L^{\star}$ for its common limit,
\eqref{eq:pool-int:sigma-xi} simplifies to
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:sigma-xi-distrib}
  \sigma^{\xi}(\bm\omega_{\gamma},\bm\omega_{q})^{2}
  \;=\;
  m(\gamma)^{2}\,\bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\Vc\bm\omega_{\gamma}
  \;+\;
  2\,m(\gamma)\, L^{\star}\,
    \bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\Vc\bm\omega_{q}
  \;+\;
  \big(1 + (L^{\star})^{2}\big)\,
    \bm\omega_{q}^{\top}\Vc\bm\omega_{q}.
\end{equation}
With matched weights $\bm\omega_{\gamma} = \bm\omega_{q} = \bm\omega$
and the chapter target aligned to the common per-sample intermediate
level ($L^{\star} = 0$), \eqref{eq:pool-int:sigma-xi-distrib}
collapses to $(1 + m(\gamma)^{2})\,\bm\omega^{\top}\Vc\bm\omega$ ---
the natural pooled analogue of the iid intermediate QB variance
$\gamma^{2}(1 + m(\gamma)^{2})$ of \Cref{thm:bg:qb-iid}.

\paragraph{Why two weight vectors.}
The separate weight pair $(\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q})$ is
needed to expose the full variance structure of the pooled QB
expectile estimator: the cross term in \eqref{eq:pool-int:sigma-xi}
couples the two pooled inputs through $\mathrm{diag}(\bm L^{\star})$
and is a genuine structural feature of the expectile target. As
\Cref{sec:pool-int:weights} shows, however, the additional freedom is
not exploited by the variance optimum in the equal-fraction
distributed-inference case: under
\Cref{cor:bg:pool-distrib-oracle} the variance-optimal pair
collapses to a single Paper~3 quantile-style weight vector
$\bm\omega^{\xi,\mathrm{Var}}_{\gamma} =
 \bm\omega^{\xi,\mathrm{Var}}_{q} = \bm\omega^{\mathrm{Var}}$
(\Cref{prop:pool-int:var-opt-distrib}).

\section{Variance- and AMSE-optimal weights}
\label{sec:pool-int:weights}

We now identify the weight vectors $(\bm\omega_{\gamma},
\bm\omega_{q})$ that minimise the asymptotic variance
\eqref{eq:pool-int:sigma-xi} and the AMSE
$(\mu^{\xi})^{2} + \sigma^{\xi, 2}$ of the pooled QB expectile
estimator, subject to the simplex constraints
$\bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\bm 1 = \bm\omega_{q}^{\top}\bm 1 = 1$.
The qualitative question driving this section is whether the
expectile-optimal weights coincide with those for the quantile target
of \Cref{prop:bg:optimal-weights}, or whether the expectile target
requires its own weight scheme.

\subsection*{Variance-optimal weights}

The variance \eqref{eq:pool-int:sigma-xi} admits the identity
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:variance-factorisation}
  \sigma^{\xi}(\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q})^{2}
  \;=\;
  \bm u^{\top}\Vc\bm u
  \;+\;
  \bm\omega_{q}^{\top}\Vc\bm\omega_{q},
  \qquad
  \bm u \;\coloneqq\; m(\gamma)\,\bm\omega_{\gamma}
       + \mathrm{diag}(\bm L^{\star})\,\bm\omega_{q},
\end{equation}
which one verifies by direct expansion using the diagonality of
$\Vc$ (and hence its commutativity with $\mathrm{diag}(\bm L^{\star})$).
Both quadratic forms in \eqref{eq:pool-int:variance-factorisation}
are individually minimised by aligning their argument with
$\Vc^{-1}\bm 1$; whether this can be done by a single choice of
$(\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q})$ depends on whether the linear
constraint induced on $\bm u$ by the simplex constraints is
sample-free. It is, precisely under the equal-fraction
distributed-inference assumption $k_{j}/n_{j} = (k/n)(1 + o(1))$ of
\Cref{cor:bg:pool-distrib-oracle}, which renders $L_{j}(\tau_{n})$
sample-free and collapses
$\mathrm{diag}(\bm L^{\star})$ to a scalar multiple of the identity.

\begin{proposition}[Variance-optimal weights, equal-fraction distributed inference]
\label{prop:pool-int:var-opt-distrib}
Under the assumptions of \Cref{thm:pool-int:main} and the
equal-sample-fraction condition
$k_{j}/n_{j} = (k/n)(1 + o(1))$ of
\Cref{cor:bg:pool-distrib-oracle}, the variance-optimal weights for
the pooled QB expectile estimator satisfy
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:var-opt-distrib-coincide}
  \bm\omega^{\xi,\mathrm{Var}}_{q}
  \;=\;
  \bm\omega^{\mathrm{Var}}
  \;=\;
  \frac{\Vc^{-1}\bm 1}{\bm 1^{\top}\Vc^{-1}\bm 1},
\end{equation}
with corresponding asymptotic variance
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:var-opt-distrib-value}
  \sigma^{\xi}\big(\bm\omega^{\xi,\mathrm{Var}}_{\gamma},
                    \bm\omega^{\xi,\mathrm{Var}}_{q}\big)^{2}
  \;=\;
  \big[(m(\gamma) + L^{\star})^{2} + 1\big]\,
  \big(\bm 1^{\top}\Vc^{-1}\bm 1\big)^{-1}.
\end{equation}
If $m(\gamma) \ne 0$, the variance-optimal pair is unique and
$\bm\omega^{\xi,\mathrm{Var}}_{\gamma} = \bm\omega^{\mathrm{Var}}$
as well, so both pooled inputs receive the Paper~3 quantile-pooling
weights. If $m(\gamma) = 0$, the asymptotic variance does not depend
on $\bm\omega_{\gamma}$: any admissible vector is then
variance-optimal, and the matched representative
$\bm\omega^{\xi,\mathrm{Var}}_{\gamma} = \bm\omega^{\mathrm{Var}}$ is
one canonical choice.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The proposition is the specialisation $\bm L^{\star} = L^{\star}\bm
1$ of \Cref{prop:pool-int:var-opt-general}; see
\Cref{app:proofs:var-opt-general}, which discharges both the
$m(\gamma) \ne 0$ closed form and the $m(\gamma) = 0$ degenerate
case.
\end{proof}

\Cref{prop:pool-int:var-opt-distrib} is the central
variance-efficiency statement of this chapter: under equal-fraction
distributed inference, \emph{the variance-optimal pooling weights are
invariant under the change of target from extreme quantile to
extreme expectile.} The two targets share the same data-fusion
recipe; the only change in the asymptotic variance is the
multiplicative factor $(m(\gamma) + L^{\star})^{2} + 1$ replacing the
quantile-target factor of $1$. The distributed-inference oracle
equivalence (\Cref{cor:bg:pool-distrib-oracle}) thus extends to the
expectile target at the variance criterion without modification of
the pooling rule. The AMSE-optimum, by contrast, is genuinely
target-dependent even under equal-fraction distributed inference, as
the second subsection below makes precise.

\paragraph{Beyond the equal-fraction setting.}
When the sample fractions $k_{j}/n_{j}$ vary across $j$,
$\mathrm{diag}(\bm L^{\star})$ ceases to be a scalar multiple of the
identity, the constraint
$\bm u^{\top}\bm 1
= m(\gamma) + \bm\omega_{q}^{\top}\bm L^{\star}$ acquires explicit
dependence on $\bm\omega_{q}$, and the decoupling argument used in
\Cref{prop:pool-int:var-opt-distrib} breaks. The variance-optimal
pair then lifts off the matched-weight diagonal and admits the
explicit closed form recorded below. Set
$\bm D \coloneqq \mathrm{diag}(\bm L^{\star})$ and abbreviate the
three scalar invariants of $(\Vc, \bm L^{\star})$:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:var-opt-abcDelta}
  a \;\coloneqq\; \bm 1^{\top}\Vc^{-1}\bm 1,
  \qquad
  b \;\coloneqq\; \bm 1^{\top}\Vc^{-1}\bm L^{\star},
  \qquad
  c \;\coloneqq\; (\bm L^{\star})^{\top}\Vc^{-1}\bm L^{\star},
  \qquad
  \Delta \;\coloneqq\; a(a + c) - b^{2}.
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}[Variance-optimal weights, general case]
\label{prop:pool-int:var-opt-general}
Under the assumptions of \Cref{thm:pool-int:main} and $m(\gamma) \ne
0$, the Cauchy--Schwarz inequality in the $\Vc^{-1}$-inner product
gives $ac - b^{2} \ge 0$, with equality iff $\bm L^{\star}$ is a
scalar multiple of $\bm 1$ --- precisely the equal-fraction case in
which $L_{j}^{\star}$ is sample-free across $j$. Consequently
\[
  \Delta \;=\; a^{2} + (ac - b^{2}) \;\ge\; a^{2} \;>\; 0,
\]
with $\Delta = a^{2}$ exactly under the equal-fraction
distributed-inference setting of
\Cref{prop:pool-int:var-opt-distrib}. The variance-optimal pooling
weights for the pooled QB intermediate expectile estimator are unique
and given by
\begin{align}
\label{eq:pool-int:var-opt-general-q}
  \bm\omega^{\xi,\mathrm{Var}}_{q}
    &\;=\;
    \frac{(a + c + m(\gamma)\,b)\,\Vc^{-1}\bm 1
          \;-\;(m(\gamma)\,a + b)\,\Vc^{-1}\bm L^{\star}}
         {\Delta},
  \\[2pt]
\label{eq:pool-int:var-opt-general-g}
  \bm\omega^{\xi,\mathrm{Var}}_{\gamma}
    &\;=\;
    \frac{(m(\gamma)\,a + b)\,\Vc^{-1}(\bm 1 + \bm D^{2}\bm 1)
          \;-\;(a + c + m(\gamma)\,b)\,\Vc^{-1}\bm L^{\star}}
         {m(\gamma)\,\Delta},
\end{align}
and the associated minimum asymptotic variance is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:var-opt-general-value}
  \sigma^{\xi}\!\big(\bm\omega^{\xi,\mathrm{Var}}_{\gamma},
                      \bm\omega^{\xi,\mathrm{Var}}_{q}\big)^{\!2}
  \;=\;
  \frac{(m(\gamma)^{2} + 1)\,a \;+\; 2\,m(\gamma)\,b \;+\; c}{\Delta}.
\end{equation}
In the distributed-inference setting $\bm L^{\star} = L^{\star}\bm 1$
one has $b = L^{\star}a$, $c = (L^{\star})^{2}a$, $\Delta = a^{2}$;
formulas
\eqref{eq:pool-int:var-opt-general-q}--\eqref{eq:pool-int:var-opt-general-value}
then collapse to
\eqref{eq:pool-int:var-opt-distrib-coincide} and
\eqref{eq:pool-int:var-opt-distrib-value}, recovering
\Cref{prop:pool-int:var-opt-distrib}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
See \Cref{app:proofs:var-opt-general}, where the change of variables
$(\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q}) \mapsto (\bm u, \bm\omega_{q})$
with $\bm u = m(\gamma)\bm\omega_{\gamma} + \bm D\bm\omega_{q}$,
positivity of $\Delta$ from Cauchy--Schwarz, and the explicit
Lagrangian dual system are spelled out step-by-step.
\end{proof}

The assumption $m(\gamma) \ne 0$ is essential for the two-vector
closed form: it is the condition that makes the change of variables
$(\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q}) \mapsto (\bm u, \bm\omega_{q})$ a
bijection, and it appears in the denominator of
\eqref{eq:pool-int:var-opt-general-g}. When $m(\gamma) = 0$ the
asymptotic variance does not identify $\bm\omega_{\gamma}$, and the
optimisation collapses to the single-vector problem in
$\bm\omega_{q}$ recorded in \Cref{app:proofs:var-opt-general}.

\paragraph{Departure from the matched-weight diagonal.}
The discrepancy between $(\bm\omega^{\xi,\mathrm{Var}}_{\gamma},
\bm\omega^{\xi,\mathrm{Var}}_{q})$ and the Paper~3 matched-weight
pair $(\bm\omega^{\mathrm{Var}}, \bm\omega^{\mathrm{Var}})$ is
controlled by the $\Vc^{-1}$-Cauchy--Schwarz defect $ac - b^{2} \ge
0$, which vanishes exactly when $\bm L^{\star}$ is a scalar multiple
of $\bm 1$. Writing $\bar L^{\star} \coloneqq b/a$ for the
$\Vc^{-1}\bm 1$-weighted mean of $\bm L^{\star}$, a direct expansion
of \eqref{eq:pool-int:var-opt-general-q} delivers the closed-form
discrepancy
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:var-opt-general-departure}
  \bm\omega^{\xi,\mathrm{Var}}_{q} - \bm\omega^{\mathrm{Var}}
  \;=\;
  -\,\frac{m(\gamma)\,a + b}{\Delta}\,
   \Vc^{-1}\big(\bm L^{\star} - \bar L^{\star}\bm 1\big),
\end{equation}
verified in \Cref{app:proofs:var-opt-general} (Step (v)), with an
analogous identity for $\bm\omega^{\xi,\mathrm{Var}}_{\gamma}$
obtained by inversion of the $\bm u$-definition. The correction lies
entirely along the $\Vc^{-1}$-centred direction
$\Vc^{-1}(\bm L^{\star} - \bar L^{\star}\bm 1)$. Writing $\Delta =
a^{2} + (ac - b^{2})$ exhibits its scalar prefactor as
\[
  \frac{m(\gamma)\,a + b}{\Delta}
  \;=\;
  \frac{m(\gamma) + \bar L^{\star}}{a}
  \cdot
  \left[1 \;+\; O\!\left(\frac{ac - b^{2}}{a^{2}}\right)\right]
\]
in the small Cauchy--Schwarz defect regime $ac - b^{2} \ll a^{2}$.
The Paper~3 weights therefore remain near-optimal whenever (i) the
log-rate vector is nearly sample-free ($ac - b^{2} \approx 0$, i.e.\
$\bm L^{\star}$ nearly constant in the $\Vc^{-1}\bm 1$-weighted
sense), or (ii) $m(\gamma) + \bar L^{\star} \approx 0$, which combines
the expectile-specific zero $\gamma^{\star} \approx 0.218$ of
$m(\,\cdot\,)$ with the log-rate weighted mean. The degenerate case $m(\gamma) = 0$
leaves $\bm\omega_{\gamma}$ indeterminate and reduces the
optimisation to the single-vector problem $\min \bm\omega_{q}^{\top}
\Vc(\bm I + \bm D^{2})\bm\omega_{q}$ subject to
$\bm\omega_{q}^{\top}\bm 1 = 1$, whose solution is recorded in
\Cref{app:proofs:var-opt-general}; it coincides with
$\bm\omega^{\mathrm{Var}}$ in the equal-fraction setting and
deviates from it otherwise.

\subsection*{AMSE-optimal weights}

The AMSE-optimal pair
$(\bm\omega^{\xi,\mathrm{AMSE}}_{\gamma},
\bm\omega^{\xi,\mathrm{AMSE}}_{q})$ minimises the asymptotic mean
squared error
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:amse-def}
  \mathrm{AMSE}^{\xi}(\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q})
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \big(\mu^{\xi}(\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q})\big)^{2}
  + \sigma^{\xi}(\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q})^{2}
\end{equation}
subject to the simplex constraints
$\bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\bm 1 = \bm\omega_{q}^{\top}\bm 1 = 1$, with
$\mu^{\xi}$ and $\sigma^{\xi}$ as in \eqref{eq:pool-int:mu-xi} and
\eqref{eq:pool-int:sigma-xi}. The objective parallels
\Cref{prop:bg:optimal-weights}(ii), but the expectile target reshapes
the bias landscape on two structural counts. First, in the two-weight
problem the bias is a linear functional of the stacked vector
$(\bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}, \bm\omega_{q}^{\top})^{\top}$ with
\emph{stacked gradient} $(m(\gamma)\Bc, \Bc^{\star})^{\top}$, in
which the pooled-Hill bias enters rescaled by the QB Jacobian
$m(\gamma)$ and the pooled-Weissman bias enters unrescaled; the
composite direction $m(\gamma)\Bc + \Bc^{\star}$ that replaces the
Paper~3 vector $\Bc^{\star}$ arises only when restricted to the
matched-weight diagonal $\bm\omega_{\gamma} = \bm\omega_{q}$. Second,
$\mu^{\xi}$ carries the additive offset $b^{\xi/\Quant}$ of
\eqref{eq:pool-int:b-xi-Q-def}, which translates the AMSE minimum
away from the Paper~3 variance-optimal direction.

\paragraph{Stacked notation.}
To state the optimum compactly, stack the two pooling weight vectors
into $\bm w \coloneqq (\bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top},
\bm\omega_{q}^{\top})^{\top} \in \R^{2m}$ and assemble the bias
gradient and variance Hessian implied by
\eqref{eq:pool-int:mu-xi}--\eqref{eq:pool-int:sigma-xi}:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:amse-stacked}
  \bm h \;\coloneqq\;
  \begin{pmatrix} m(\gamma)\,\Bc \\[2pt] \Bc^{\star} \end{pmatrix}
  \in \R^{2m},
  \qquad
  \bm Q \;\coloneqq\;
  \begin{pmatrix}
    m(\gamma)^{2}\,\Vc & m(\gamma)\,\bm D\,\Vc \\[2pt]
    m(\gamma)\,\bm D\,\Vc & \Vc + \bm D^{2}\Vc
  \end{pmatrix}
  \in \R^{2m \times 2m},
\end{equation}
so that
$\mu^{\xi}(\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q}) = \bm h^{\top}\bm w +
b^{\xi/\Quant}$ and
$\sigma^{\xi}(\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q})^{2} = \bm w^{\top}
\bm Q\bm w$. Write $\bm A \in \R^{2m \times 2}$ for the matrix with
columns $\bm 1_{g} \coloneqq (\bm 1^{\top}, \bm 0^{\top})^{\top}$ and
$\bm 1_{q} \coloneqq (\bm 0^{\top}, \bm 1^{\top})^{\top}$, so that the
simplex constraints read $\bm A^{\top}\bm w = \bm 1_{2}$.

\begin{proposition}[AMSE-optimal weights]
\label{prop:pool-int:amse-opt}
Under the assumptions of \Cref{thm:pool-int:main} and $m(\gamma) \ne
0$, the matrix $\bm M \coloneqq \bm Q + \bm h\bm h^{\top}$ is
positive definite, and the AMSE objective \eqref{eq:pool-int:amse-def}
is strictly convex on the affine constraint set
$\{\bm w \in \R^{2m} : \bm A^{\top}\bm w = \bm 1_{2}\}$. The unique
minimiser admits the closed form
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:amse-opt-formula}
  \bm w^{\xi,\mathrm{AMSE}}
  \;=\;
  \bm M^{-1}\!\left[\bm A\,(\bm A^{\top}\bm M^{-1}\bm A)^{-1}\!
                     \big(\bm 1_{2} \;+\; b^{\xi/\Quant}\,
                          \bm A^{\top}\bm M^{-1}\bm h\big)
                    \;-\; b^{\xi/\Quant}\,\bm h\right],
\end{equation}
and is equivalently characterised by the first-order conditions
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:amse-foc}
\begin{aligned}
  m(\gamma)^{2}\,\Vc\,\bm\omega_{\gamma}
  + m(\gamma)\,\bm D\,\Vc\,\bm\omega_{q}
  &\;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\lambda_{\gamma}\bm 1
       \;-\; m(\gamma)\,\mu^{\xi}\,\Bc, \\
  m(\gamma)\,\bm D\,\Vc\,\bm\omega_{\gamma}
  + \big(\Vc + \bm D^{2}\Vc\big)\,\bm\omega_{q}
  &\;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\lambda_{q}\bm 1
       \;-\; \mu^{\xi}\,\Bc^{\star},
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
together with the simplex constraints
$\bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\bm 1 = \bm\omega_{q}^{\top}\bm 1 = 1$ and
the self-referential identity
$\mu^{\xi} = m(\gamma)\,\bm\omega_{\gamma}^{\top}\Bc +
\bm\omega_{q}^{\top}\Bc^{\star} + b^{\xi/\Quant}$. The Lagrange
multipliers $\lambda_{\gamma}, \lambda_{q} \in \R$ are uniquely
determined by these constraints.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
See \Cref{app:proofs:amse-opt}, where positive-definiteness of $\bm
Q$ (and hence of $\bm M = \bm Q + \bm h\bm h^{\top}$) is verified in
Step (ii) via the variance factorisation, the Lagrangian and
explicit FOC normalisation are recorded in Step (iii), and the block
decomposition matching \eqref{eq:pool-int:amse-foc} is in Step (iv).
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Comparison with the Paper~3 AMSE optimum.}
The expectile AMSE-optimum does \emph{not} reduce to the Paper~3
quantile AMSE-optimum of \Cref{prop:bg:optimal-weights}(ii), even
under the equal-fraction distributed-inference simplification
$\bm D = L^{\star}\bm I$ that aligned the two variance-optima in
\Cref{prop:pool-int:var-opt-distrib}. Three structural differences
persist:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
  \item the bias gradient is the stacked vector
        $\bm h = (m(\gamma)\Bc, \Bc^{\star})^{\top}$ rather than the
        single-block $\Bc^{\star}$ of \Cref{prop:bg:optimal-weights};
  \item $\mu^{\xi}$ carries the additive offset
        $b^{\xi/\Quant}$, which shifts the AMSE minimum away from
        the variance-optimal direction $\Vc^{-1}\bm 1$;
  \item the expectile minimiser is genuinely a pair
        $(\bm\omega_{\gamma}, \bm\omega_{q})$, and the diagonal
        $\bm\omega_{\gamma} = \bm\omega_{q}$ is generically not a
        solution to the FOC \eqref{eq:pool-int:amse-foc} because the
        Hill and Weissman blocks of $\bm h$ enter asymmetrically
        through $m(\gamma)\Bc$ and $\Bc^{\star}$.
\end{enumerate}
The two AMSE-optima coincide only under restrictive alignment
conditions; a transparent sufficient case is $b^{\xi/\Quant} = 0$
together with the structural alignment
$\Bc^{\star} \propto m(\gamma)\,\Bc$ --- the deterministic
expectile--quantile gap must vanish at the chapter rate, and the
pooled-Hill and pooled-Weissman biases must reinforce each other in
proportion to the QB Jacobian. We do not characterise the exact
necessary condition: it would require inverting the closed-form
\eqref{eq:pool-int:amse-opt-formula} against the Paper~3 expression
and isolating the locus of coincidence in
$(\Vc, \bm D, \Bc, \Bc^{\star}, b^{\xi/\Quant})$-space, a calculation
that is not generically a single algebraic constraint. Generically,
however, the AMSE-optimum is target-dependent. This asymmetry
between the variance-optimum, which is target-invariant under
equal-fraction distributed inference
(\Cref{prop:pool-int:var-opt-distrib}), and the AMSE-optimum, which
is target-dependent even there, is the qualitative signature of
carrying the expectile target through the QB plug-in.

\section{Estimated weights and confidence intervals}
\label{sec:pool-int:CI}

The variance-optimal weights of
\Cref{prop:pool-int:var-opt-distrib,prop:pool-int:var-opt-general}
depend on the population covariance $\Vc$ alone, while the
AMSE-optimal weights of \Cref{prop:pool-int:amse-opt} additionally
depend on the bias quantities $\Bc, \Bc^{\star}$ and on the
deterministic expectile--quantile offset $b^{\xi/\Quant}$. All of
these are unknown in practice. Following the plug-in scheme of
\textcite[\S2.2]{DaouiaPadoanStupfler2021} recalled in
\Cref{sec:bg:pooling}, we estimate $\Vc$ and $\Bc$ by consistent
empirical analogues $\hatVc, \hatBc$ built from the per-sample
$\hat\gamma_{j}(k_{j})$ and from estimators
$\hat\beta_{j}, \hat\rho_{j}$ of the second-order parameters of
$\mathcal C_{2}(\gamma, \rho, A)$; $\Bc^{\star}$ admits the analogous
plug-in $\hatBc^{\star}$ via \eqref{eq:pool-int:Bcstar-def}, and
$b^{\xi/\Quant}$ via \eqref{eq:pool-int:b-xi-Q-def}. We additionally
plug in $\hat m \coloneqq m(\hat\gamma_{n}(\hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma}))$
for $m(\gamma)$ in \eqref{eq:pool-int:sigma-xi}. Continuity of
$m(\,\cdot\,)$, of the optimal-weight maps in
\Cref{prop:bg:optimal-weights,prop:pool-int:var-opt-general,prop:pool-int:amse-opt},
and of the variance form \eqref{eq:pool-int:sigma-xi} ensures that
the plug-in estimators
$\hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma}, \hat{\bm\omega}_{q}, \hat m,
\hat\sigma^{\xi}$ are consistent for their population analogues, and
that the composite estimator
$\hatexpectile{\tau_{n}}^{\mathrm{pool}}(\hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma},
\hat{\bm\omega}_{q})$ is $\sqrt{k}$-asymptotically equivalent to
$\hatexpectile{\tau_{n}}^{\mathrm{pool}}(\bm\omega_{\gamma},
\bm\omega_{q})$ \parencite[Theorem~3]{DaouiaPadoanStupfler2021}.

\paragraph{Variance versus AMSE feasibility.}
The variance-optimal plug-in requires only $\hatVc$ --- a consistent
estimator of a first-order quantity --- whereas the AMSE-optimal
plug-in additionally requires consistent estimation of $\Bc$,
$\Bc^{\star}$, and the expectile--quantile offset $b^{\xi/\Quant}$,
all of which are second-order quantities driven by the unknown rate
function $A(\,\cdot\,)$ and the second-order parameter $\rho$.
Estimating second-order quantities in the heavy-tailed regime is
notoriously delicate
\parencite[\S5.2]{deHaanFerreira2006}, and the AMSE-optimal weights
are consequently substantially less stable in finite samples than
the variance-optimal weights. For this reason the variance-optimal
plug-in is the more robust default in practice; the AMSE-optimal
plug-in is best reserved for settings where reliable estimators of
$\rho$ and $A$ are available.

\begin{corollary}[Asymptotic confidence interval for $\expectile{\tau_{n}}$]
\label{cor:pool-int:CI}
Assume \Cref{thm:pool-int:main}, the consistency conditions
$\hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma} \toprob \bm\omega_{\gamma}$,
$\hat{\bm\omega}_{q} \toprob \bm\omega_{q}$, $\hat m \toprob m(\gamma)$,
$\hatVc \toprob \Vc$ in the operator norm, and
$\sqrt{k}\,\hat\mu^{\xi}(\hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma},
\hat{\bm\omega}_{q}) \toprob 0$ (asymptotically negligible bias).
Then an asymptotic $(1 - \alpha)$ confidence interval for
$\expectile{\tau_{n}}$ on the log scale is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:CI-log}
  \log \hatexpectile{\tau_{n}}^{\mathrm{pool}}\!
       (\hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma}, \hat{\bm\omega}_{q})
  \;\pm\;
  z_{1 - \alpha/2}\,
  \frac{\hat\sigma^{\xi}(\hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma},
                         \hat{\bm\omega}_{q})}{\sqrt{k}},
\end{equation}
where $z_{1 - \alpha/2}$ is the standard-normal quantile of order
$1 - \alpha/2$ and $\hat\sigma^{\xi}$ is the plug-in version of
\eqref{eq:pool-int:sigma-xi}. The corresponding interval on the
original scale is the exponential of \eqref{eq:pool-int:CI-log}.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}[Sketch.]
Slutsky's theorem applied to \eqref{eq:pool-int:main-clt} delivers
the asymptotic pivot on the log scale; the continuous-mapping
theorem applied to $\exp$ transports the interval to the original
scale.
\end{proof}

Three practical remarks. First, the log-scale interval
\eqref{eq:pool-int:CI-log} is symmetric in $\log \expectile{\tau_{n}}$
while its exponential is not; the latter is the natural form for
applications in which $\expectile{\tau_{n}}$ is interpreted as a
monetary risk measure. Second, under the equal-fraction
distributed-inference setting of
\Cref{prop:pool-int:var-opt-distrib} the variance-optimal weights are
identical across the two pooled inputs, so a single estimated
$\hat{\bm\omega}^{\mathrm{Var}} = \hatVc^{-1}\bm 1 /
(\bm 1^{\top}\hatVc^{-1}\bm 1)$ suffices and the plug-in variance
simplifies to
$\hat\sigma^{\xi, 2}
= \big[(\hat m + \hat L^{\star})^{2} + 1\big]
/ (\bm 1^{\top}\hatVc^{-1}\bm 1)$,
where $\hat L^{\star} = \log(k/(n(1 - \tau_{n})))$.

Third, the interval \eqref{eq:pool-int:CI-log} is a
\emph{first-order variance-based} interval, valid only when the
$\sqrt{k}$-bias of the pooled estimator is asymptotically negligible
--- the explicit assumption
$\sqrt{k}\,\hat\mu^{\xi}(\hat{\bm\omega}_{\gamma},
\hat{\bm\omega}_{q}) \toprob 0$ in
\Cref{cor:pool-int:CI}. \Cref{thm:pool-int:main} shows that this is
typically not the case: $\mu^{\xi}$ involves the second-order
quantities $\Bc, \Bc^{\star}$ and the offset $b^{\xi/\Quant}$, all of
which generically contribute non-zero asymptotic bias under
$\mathcal C_{2}(\gamma, \rho, A)$. When this bias is not negligible,
\eqref{eq:pool-int:CI-log} should either be re-centred on a
bias-corrected point estimator
$\hatexpectile{\tau_{n}}^{\mathrm{pool}} \exp\!\big(-
\hat\mu^{\xi}/\sqrt{k}\big)$, or its width should incorporate a
plug-in estimate of $\mu^{\xi}/\sqrt{k}$ as a deterministic shift.
Standard practice in the extreme-value literature selects $k$ small
enough that the bias contribution sits below the CLT noise, which
amounts to operating away from the AMSE-optimal $k$ of
\Cref{prop:pool-int:amse-opt} --- another reason the variance-optimal
plug-in is the more natural default for inference.

\section{A test of expectile-tail-homogeneity}
\label{sec:pool-int:test}

The variance-optimal pooling of \Cref{prop:pool-int:var-opt-distrib}
rests on the common-$\gamma$ assumption \eqref{eq:bg:common-gamma}
across the $m$ samples. Although the chapter assumes a common
marginal $F$ throughout, in practice this assumption ought to be
validated before pooling. The chi-square test of
\Cref{thm:bg:tail-homogeneity-test} provides such a diagnostic at the
tail-index level; we adapt it here to a target formulated directly in
terms of the expectile, which is the quantity actually being
inferred.

Let
\[
  \hat\xi_{j}(\tau_{n})
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \psifn\!\big(\hat\gamma_{j}(k_{j})\big)\,
  \hat q^{\star}_{j}(1 - \tau_{n} \mid k_{j}),
  \qquad j = 1, \ldots, m,
\]
denote the per-sample QB intermediate-expectile estimators. Under
the common-marginal assumption, all $\hat\xi_{j}(\tau_{n})$ estimate
the same population $\expectile{\tau_{n}}$, and their log-scale
dispersion is captured by the GLS deviance statistic
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pool-int:test-stat}
  \Lambda^{\xi}_{n}
  \;\coloneqq\;
  k\,\big(\hat{\bm\Xi}_{n} - \hat\nu_{n}\bm 1\big)^{\!\top}
    \hat{\bm\Sigma}_{n}^{-1}
    \big(\hat{\bm\Xi}_{n} - \hat\nu_{n}\bm 1\big),
  \qquad
  \hat\nu_{n}
  \;=\;
  \frac{\bm 1^{\top}\hat{\bm\Sigma}_{n}^{-1}\hat{\bm\Xi}_{n}}
       {\bm 1^{\top}\hat{\bm\Sigma}_{n}^{-1}\bm 1},
\end{equation}
where
$\hat{\bm\Xi}_{n} = \big(\log\hat\xi_{1}(\tau_{n}), \ldots,
\log\hat\xi_{m}(\tau_{n})\big)^{\top}$ and
$\hat{\bm\Sigma}_{n}$ is a consistent estimator of the
$\sqrt{k}$-asymptotic covariance matrix of
$\hat{\bm\Xi}_{n} - \log\expectile{\tau_{n}}\bm 1$ derived in the
proof appendix.

\begin{theorem}[Expectile-tail-homogeneity test]
\label{thm:pool-int:hom-test}
Under the assumptions of \Cref{thm:pool-int:main} and the null
hypothesis
\[
  H_{0}^{\xi}\colon\quad
  \frac{\expectile{\tau_{n},\,j}}{\expectile{\tau_{n},\,\ell}}
  \;\to\; 1
  \qquad \text{for every } j \ne \ell,
\]
the statistic \eqref{eq:pool-int:test-stat} satisfies
$\Lambda^{\xi}_{n} \todist \chi^{2}_{m - 1}$; under any alternative,
$\Lambda^{\xi}_{n} \toprob \infty$. A test of asymptotic size
$\alpha$ rejects $H_{0}^{\xi}$ when
$\Lambda^{\xi}_{n} > \chi^{2}_{m - 1, 1 - \alpha}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Sketch.]
Per-sample joint $\sqrt{k_{j}}$-CLTs from
\eqref{eq:pool-int:per-sample-clt}, aggregated across samples under
independence, deliver
$\sqrt{k}\big(\hat{\bm\Xi}_{n} - \log\expectile{\tau_{n}}\bm 1\big)
\todist \mathcal N(\bm 0, \bm\Sigma)$ under $H_{0}^{\xi}$, with
$\bm\Sigma$ assembled from the within-sample $2 \times 2$ blocks of
\eqref{eq:pool-int:per-sample-clt} read off through the
expectile-specific row $(m(\gamma), 1)$ (cf.\ the proof sketch of
\Cref{thm:pool-int:main}). The Wald-type statistic
\eqref{eq:pool-int:test-stat} is the standard chi-square deviance
about its GLS-weighted mean, and its $\chi^{2}_{m - 1}$ limit
follows from $\hat{\bm\Sigma}_{n} \toprob \bm\Sigma$. Consistency
under the alternative is a consequence of the divergence of
$\hat{\bm\Xi}_{n} - \hat\nu_{n}\bm 1$ when the population expectiles
diverge.
\end{proof}

Two remarks. First, under the chapter's standing assumption of a
common marginal $F$, $H_{0}^{\xi}$ is automatically satisfied; the
test serves to validate the assumption on the data, and a
non-rejection licenses the use of the pooling recipes of the
preceding sections. Second, under the tail-homoskedasticity condition
$(\mathcal H)$ of \Cref{cond:bg:tail-homoskedasticity}, the
expectile null $H_{0}^{\xi}$ is equivalent to the tail-index null
$H_{0}: \gamma_{1} = \cdots = \gamma_{m}$ of
\Cref{thm:bg:tail-homogeneity-test}; the two tests then check
overlapping hypotheses, with $\Lambda^{\xi}_{n}$ enjoying greater
power against deviations that manifest primarily at the expectile
level rather than at the level of the tail index alone.
